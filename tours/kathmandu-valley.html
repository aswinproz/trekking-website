About the Kathmandu Valley Tour

Embark on a captivating 3-day journey through the heart of Nepal's cultural heritage with our comprehensive Kathmandu Valley Tour. This meticulously designed tour takes you through the ancient streets and sacred temples that have made Kathmandu one of the world's most fascinating cultural destinations. As you explore the seven UNESCO World Heritage Sites scattered throughout the valley, you'll witness the perfect blend of Hindu and Buddhist traditions that define Nepal's unique spiritual identity.

The tour begins with your arrival in Kathmandu, where you'll be greeted with a traditional welcome dinner featuring authentic Nepali cuisine and cultural performances. Over the next three days, you'll visit some of the most revered religious sites in South Asia, including the sacred Pashupatinath Temple, the magnificent Boudhanath Stupa, the ancient Swayambhunath (also known as Monkey Temple), and the beautifully preserved Patan and Kathmandu Durbar Squares.

Each site tells a story of centuries-old traditions, exquisite Newari architecture, and the deep-rooted spirituality that permeates every corner of the Kathmandu Valley. Our experienced guides will provide insightful commentary about the historical significance, religious importance, and cultural practices associated with each location, ensuring you gain a profound understanding of Nepal's rich heritage.

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1

Arrival in Kathmandu

Upon your arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu, you will be warmly greeted by our representative and transferred to your hotel. After settling in, you'll receive a comprehensive tour briefing and have time to rest and freshen up.

In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner at a traditional Nepali restaurant, featuring authentic cuisine from various regions of Nepal. The dinner is accompanied by a cultural program showcasing traditional Nepali dances, including the famous Mask Dance and folk performances. This is a wonderful opportunity to immerse yourself in Nepal's vibrant cultural heritage right from the start of your journey.

Overnight in Kathmandu with breakfast included the next morning.

Day 2

Full Day Sightseeing Tour

After an early breakfast, set out for a comprehensive full-day sightseeing tour of the Kathmandu Valley's most sacred and historically significant sites. Your private vehicle and guide will take you to each location, ensuring a comfortable and informative experience.

Pashupatinath Temple

Begin your day at Pashupatinath Temple, one of the most sacred Hindu temples in the world and the principal shrine of Lord Shiva. Located on the banks of the holy Bagmati River, this temple complex dates back to at least 400 BC. Witness the evening aarti ceremony and observe Hindu pilgrims performing ritual baths and cremation rites along the river ghats. The temple's pagoda-style architecture and intricate wood carvings are masterpieces of Newari craftsmanship.

Boudhanath Stupa

Next, visit the magnificent Boudhanath Stupa, one of the largest spherical stupas in the world and a major pilgrimage site for Buddhists. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is adorned with 108 images of the Buddha and countless prayer flags. Circumambulate the stupa clockwise while spinning the prayer wheels to earn spiritual merit. The surrounding monastery houses Tibetan monks and offers excellent opportunities to purchase Buddhist artifacts and thangka paintings.

Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple)

Continue to Swayambhunath, the ancient religious site perched atop a hillock in the northwest of Kathmandu. This sacred Buddhist stupa is surrounded by a complex of shrines, temples, and monasteries. The journey to the top involves climbing 365 stone steps, but the panoramic views of the Kathmandu Valley from the summit are well worth the effort. The temple is home to numerous monkeys, believed to be holy, giving it the nickname "Monkey Temple."

Patan Durbar Square

End the day at Patan Durbar Square, a UNESCO World Heritage Site showcasing the finest examples of Newari architecture. The square is home to the Royal Palace, the Temple of Lord Krishna, and numerous other temples and monuments. Visit the Patan Museum, one of the best museums in South Asia for Hindu and Buddhist art. Marvel at the exquisite stone carvings, bronze works, and wooden sculptures that adorn every corner of this ancient royal complex.

Return to your hotel for overnight stay.

Day 3

Kathmandu Durbar Square - Shopping - Departure

After breakfast, visit Kathmandu Durbar Square, also known as Hanuman Dhoka Palace Complex. This UNESCO World Heritage Site was the royal palace of the Malla kings and is a testament to the architectural genius of the Newar craftsmen. Explore the intricate wooden carvings of the Kasthamandap temple, the majestic Taleju Temple, and the Kumari Ghar, home to the living goddess Kumari.

After exploring the Durbar Square, take some time to browse the traditional markets and antique shops in the surrounding area. Kathmandu offers excellent opportunities for shopping, from handwoven carpets and pashmina shawls to Buddhist thangkas, silver jewelry, and traditional mask reproductions. Don't miss the chance to bargain for souvenirs at the local markets.

Depending on your flight schedule, you will be transferred to Tribhuvan International Airport for your departure. Your guide will assist with check-in procedures and ensure you have a smooth departure, marking the end of your unforgettable Kathmandu Valley Tour.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the UNESCO World Heritage Sites included in the Kathmandu Valley Tour?

The Kathmandu Valley Tour includes four UNESCO World Heritage Sites: Pashupatinath Temple (Hindu sacred site), Boudhanath Stupa (Buddhist pilgrimage), Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple), and both Patan Durbar Square and Kathmandu Durbar Square. These sites represent the pinnacle of Newari architecture and religious significance in Nepal.

What is the best time to do the Kathmandu Valley Tour?

The Kathmandu Valley Tour can be done all year round. However, the best time is during spring (March to May) when the weather is pleasant and flowers are in bloom, and autumn (September to November) for clear skies and comfortable temperatures. Monsoon season (June to August) brings occasional rains but fewer crowds. Winter (December to February) can be cold but offers a unique experience with fewer tourists.

Do I need any permits for the Kathmandu Valley Tour?

No special permits are required for the Kathmandu Valley Tour as all sites are within the city. However, temple entry fees are included in your tour package. Your guide will handle all entrance arrangements. For Nepal entry, you'll need a visa which can be obtained on arrival at Kathmandu airport or applied for online in advance.

What should I wear when visiting temples in Kathmandu?

When visiting Hindu temples like Pashupatinath, it's important to dress modestly - covering shoulders and knees. Light, breathable clothing is recommended as you'll be walking between sites. Comfortable walking shoes are essential. For Buddhist sites, casual but respectful attire is appropriate. Remove shoes before entering certain temples and shrines.

Is the Kathmandu Valley Tour suitable for children and elderly travelers?

Yes, the Kathmandu Valley Tour is classified as Easy difficulty and is suitable for travelers of all ages including children and elderly visitors. The tour involves walking between temples and exploring squares, but can be adapted to a comfortable pace. Private vehicles are available for transfers between sites. However, some areas require climbing stairs, so please inform us in advance if mobility assistance is needed.

What is included in the Kathmandu Valley Tour price?

Excluded are: international flights, Nepal visa fees, personal expenses, and tips for guide and driver.
