Your Adventure Journey

Day-by-Day Itinerary

A carefully planned 4-5 day adventure from Kathmandu to Pokhara and back.

Day 1

Kathmandu to Pokhara

Depart Kathmandu early morning for the scenic drive to Pokhara via the Prithvi Highway. Enjoy the beautiful landscapes of hills, rivers, and terraced fields. En route, visit the famous Manakamana Temple (optional cable car ride) for those interested in the sacred Hindu shrine. After checking into your hotel in Pokhara, take an evening stroll along the beautiful Lakeside area, enjoying the views of Phewa Lake and the mountains. Overnight in Pokhara.

Day 2

Pokhara Sightseeing

After breakfast, visit Devi's Falls (Patale Chhango), where the water disappears into an underground tunnel. Next, explore Gupteshwor Cave, a sacred limestone cave housing a Shiva lingam with impressive stalactite and stalagmite formations. Visit the Seti River gorge, running through the middle of Pokhara. Continue to the Bindhyabasini Temple, an important Hindu shrine. Afternoon visit to the Tibetan Refugee Camp to learn about Tibetan culture and purchase handcrafted souvenirs. Evening free for Lakeside relaxation. Overnight in Pokhara.

Day 3

Adventure & Lake Day

Wake up very early for a jeep ride to Sarankot (1,600m) to witness a spectacular sunrise over the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri mountain ranges. Return to hotel for breakfast. Mid-morning, experience the thrill of tandem paragliding over Phewa Lake with breathtaking mountain views. After lunch, enjoy a peaceful boat ride on Phewa Lake to the island temple of Tal Barahi. Afternoon at leisure - explore the Lakeside market, enjoy a coffee at a lakeside cafe, or relax at your hotel. Overnight in Pokhara.

Day 4

Free Day & Optional Activities

This day is free for optional adventure activities or relaxation. Optional activities include: ultra-light flying (aerial tour of the valley), zip-lining (one of the world's fastest), mountain biking, kayaking, rock climbing, or visiting the interesting International Mountain Museum. Alternatively, explore more of Pokhara's cafes, shops, and spas, or simply relax by Phewa Lake. In the late afternoon, begin your return journey to Kathmandu. Overnight in Kathmandu (or optional stay in Pokhara).

Day 5

Departure

After breakfast, enjoy free time for last-minute shopping or additional sightseeing in Kathmandu. Optional morning visit to any remaining heritage sites or markets. Our representative will transfer you to Tribhuvan International Airport for your departure flight. Bid farewell to Nepal with unforgettable memories of your Pokhara adventure!

Explore the Wonders

Pokhara's Must-See Attractions

Discover the natural and cultural highlights of Nepal's most beautiful city.

Devi's Falls

Also known as Patale Chhango, this 30-meter waterfall is named after a goddess and features water that disappears into an underground tunnel. Best viewed during monsoon season when water flow is at its peak.

Gupteshwor Cave

A sacred limestone cave located near Devi's Falls, housing a revered Shiva lingam. The cave features impressive stalactite and stalagmite formations and requires some climbing to explore fully.

Phewa Lake

The second largest lake in Nepal, spanning 4.4 sq km. The lake reflects the Annapurna range, and the iconic Tal Barahi Temple sits on an island in the middle. Boating and kayaking are popular activities.

Sarankot

A popular hill station at 1,600m offering panoramic views of the Annapurna, Dhaulagiri, and Manaslu mountain ranges. Famous for sunrise and sunset views over the mountains.

Seti River Gorge

A dramatic gorge running through the middle of Pokhara, with the river disappearing underground. The gorge is visible from various points in the city and offers stunning views.

Tal Barahi Temple

A two-story pagoda temple dedicated to the goddess Durga, located on an island in Phewa Lake. The temple is a sacred pilgrimage site and offers beautiful views from the lake.

Questions Answered

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about the Kathmandu to Pokhara Adventure.

How far is Pokhara from Kathmandu and what is the best way to travel?

Pokhara is approximately 200 kilometers from Kathmandu, about a 6-7 hour drive via the scenic Prithvi Highway. The most popular option is a tourist bus which takes about 6-7 hours and costs around $10-15. Private car/jeep hire takes 5-6 hours and offers more comfort. For those short on time, 25-minute domestic flights are available. The drive itself is scenic, passing through hills, rivers, and small villages.

What adventure activities are available in Pokhara?

Pokhara is Nepal's adventure capital offering numerous activities: paragliding (flying over the lake with views of the Annapurna range), zip-lining (one of the world's fastest ziplines), ultra-light flying (aerial tour of the valley), bungee jumping, rock climbing, mountain biking, kayaking on Phewa Lake, and trekking to nearby viewpoints.

What is the weather like in Pokhara compared to Kathmandu?

Pokhara has a subtropical climate and is generally warmer than Kathmandu. Summer (June-August) can be hot and humid with monsoon rains. Winter (December-February) is mild and pleasant during the day. Spring (March-May) offers warm temperatures and clear skies. The best time is October-November when rainfall is minimal and visibility is excellent.

Is it safe to paraglide in Pokhara?

Paragliding in Pokhara is generally very safe when done with licensed operators. The activity is conducted by experienced, certified pilots with good safety records. Flights are weather-dependent, and operators will cancel if conditions are unsafe. Most flights last 20-30 minutes, and tandem flights are available for beginners. The stunning views make this a must-try experience.

What should I wear for a visit to Pokhara?

Pack comfortable, casual clothing suitable for warm weather. Bring light layers for mornings and evenings. For temple visits, dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees). Bring swimwear if you plan to swim in Phewa Lake or use resort pools. Sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat are essential year-round.

Can I extend my trip to include trekking from Pokhara?

Absolutely! Pokhara is the gateway to many popular treks including the Annapurna Circuit, Ghorepani Poon Hill, and the Annapurna Base Camp trek. We can easily extend your trip to include a trekking component. Many travelers choose to do a short 2-3 day trek before or after their Pokhara stay.
