Your Wild Journey

Day-by-Day Itinerary

A carefully planned 4-5 day adventure to experience the best of Nepal's wildlife.

Day 1

Arrival in Chitwan

Depart Kathmandu early morning for the scenic 5-6 hour drive to Chitwan National Park (or take a 30-minute flight to Bharatpur). Arrive at your jungle resort and enjoy welcome drinks. After lunch, attend an orientation program about the park and its wildlife. In the late afternoon, take a village walk to experience the local Tharu culture. End the day with a beautiful sunset view over the grasslands. Overnight at jungle resort in Chitwan.

Day 2

Full Day Jungle Activities

Wake up early for an exciting morning jeep safari into the depths of Chitwan National Park. Search for one-horned rhinos, deer, monkeys, and if lucky, a Bengal tiger. Return to the resort for breakfast. After lunch, experience an elephant safari through the tall grasslands, getting close to rhinos and other wildlife. Later, take a canoe ride on the Rapti River, spotting marsh mugger crocodiles and various water birds. Evening visit to the elephant breeding center. Overnight at jungle resort.

Day 3

Nature Exploration

Early morning bird watching excursion with an expert ornithologist. Spot over 400 species of birds including kingfishers, hornbills, storks, and herons. After breakfast, explore the jungle on foot with a trained nature guide, learning about plant life, insects, and smaller wildlife. Visit the Tharu Cultural Museum to understand the indigenous people of the region. Afternoon at leisure - enjoy the resort amenities or take another short jungle walk. Evening cultural program with traditional Tharu dance performances. Overnight at jungle resort.

Day 4

Optional Bardia Extension or Final Safari

Option A: Take an early morning jeep safari, then depart for Bardia National Park (5-hour drive) for an afternoon safari in this wilder, less-crowded park known for tigers. Option B: After breakfast, depart for Kathmandu with a stop at the Manakamana Temple (cable car optional). Evening arrival in Kathmandu. Overnight in Kathmandu (optional extension to Bardia available).

Day 5

Departure

After breakfast, depending on your schedule, either continue with additional safari activities or depart for Kathmandu/your next destination. Our representative will ensure smooth transfers. If flying, the 30-minute flight from Bharatpur offers stunning aerial views of the Himalayas and Terai landscape. Bid farewell to Nepal with unforgettable wildlife memories!

Explore the Wilderness

Nepal's National Parks

Discover the diverse ecosystems and wildlife of Nepal's premier protected areas.

Chitwan National Park

UNESCO World Heritage Site

The oldest national park in Nepal, covering 932 sq km of forests, grasslands, and wetlands. Famous for successful rhinoceros conservation and diverse wildlife including tigers, elephants, and over 500 bird species. Most accessible park with excellent infrastructure and activities.

Bardia National Park

Remote Wilderness

The largest national park in the Terai, covering 968 sq km of pristine forest. Known for the highest tiger population in Nepal and Gangetic dolphin sightings. Less crowded than Chitwan, offering a wilder, more authentic jungle experience. Excellent for tiger tracking.

Koshi Tappu Wildlife Reserve

Bird Watcher's Paradise

A bird sanctuary famous for over 400 species of birds including the great hornbill, Bengal florican, and numerous migratory waterfowl. Located at the confluence of three rivers, it offers excellent opportunities for bird photography and river-based activities.

Questions Answered

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about the Nepal Wildlife Safari.

What wildlife can I expect to see in Nepal's national parks?

Nepal's national parks are home to incredible biodiversity. In Chitwan, you can spot one-horned rhinoceros, Bengal tigers (though sighting is rare), elephants, gaur (Indian bison), deer species (sambar, chital, barking deer), monkeys, and over 500 species of birds. Bardia is known for tigers, rhinos, and Gangetic dolphins. Koshi Tappu is a bird-watcher's paradise with over 400 species including the great hornbill, storks, herons, and migratory birds.

What is the difference between Chitwan, Bardia, and Koshi Tappu?

Chitwan is the most popular and accessible park, ideal for first-time visitors. It offers excellent rhino sightings, elephant safaris, and diverse activities. Bardia is more remote and less crowded, offering better tiger sighting opportunities and a wilder experience. Koshi Tappu is a bird-watching paradise, famous for waterfowl and migratory birds.

Is elephant riding ethical in Nepal?

We prioritize ethical wildlife experiences. Our partner resorts use elephants that are well-cared for and not subjected to harsh training methods. We offer both elephant-based and jeep-based safaris, allowing you to choose. We encourage elephant observation in their natural habitat rather than riding when possible. Our guides will explain the conservation efforts and elephant welfare programs at the parks.

What should I wear for a jungle safari?

Wear neutral-colored, comfortable clothing that blends with the environment. Avoid bright colors and strong perfumes which can disturb wildlife. Recommended: long-sleeved shirts and pants, comfortable walking shoes or sandals, a hat, and sunglasses. Early morning safaris can be cool, so bring a layer. Neutral colors like khaki, olive, or earth tones are best.

What is the best time of year for wildlife viewing in Nepal?

The best time for wildlife viewing is from October to March when the weather is dry and animals congregate near water sources. November to February offers pleasant temperatures and excellent visibility. March-May is hot but good for bird watching when migratory birds are present. Tiger sighting is best in Bardia during the dry season.

How safe is a jungle safari in Nepal?

Jungle safaris in Nepal are generally very safe when conducted with licensed operators and experienced guides. Our guides are trained in wildlife safety and park protocols. Safari activities follow strict safety guidelines - visitors are always accompanied by trained naturalists. Following your guide's instructions ensures a safe and enjoyable experience.
