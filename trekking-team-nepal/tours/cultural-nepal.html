Your Cultural Journey

Day-by-Day Itinerary

A carefully planned 5-6 day tour to experience the best of Nepal's cultural heritage.

Day 1

Arrival in Kathmandu

Arrive at Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu. Our representative will greet you and transfer to your hotel. In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner with a traditional Nepali cultural show featuring folk dances and music. Overnight in Kathmandu.

Day 2

Kathmandu Heritage Sites

After breakfast, visit Pashupatinath Temple, one of the most sacred Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, located on the banks of the Bagmati River. Continue to Boudhanath Stupa, one of the largest spherical stupas in the world and a major pilgrimage center for Buddhists. Afternoon visit to Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple), an ancient religious site atop a hill with sweeping views of Kathmandu Valley. Evening at leisure in Thamel. Overnight in Kathmandu.

Day 3

Patan & Bhaktapur Tour

Full day excursion to the ancient cities of Patan and Bhaktapur. Start with Patan Durbar Square, also known as the "City of Fine Arts," featuring exquisite temples, courtyards, and the famous Krishna Temple. Visit the Patan Museum to understand Newari art and history. After lunch, travel to Bhaktapur, the "City of Devotees," famous for its well-preserved medieval architecture. Explore Bhaktapur Durbar Square, Nyatapola Temple (the tallest pagoda in Nepal), and the pottery square. Return to Kathmandu in the evening. Overnight in Kathmandu.

Day 4

Traditional Experiences

Today focuses on authentic cultural experiences. Start with a Newari cooking class in a local home, where you'll learn to prepare traditional dishes like Yomari, Chatamari, and various curries. After lunch, visit a traditional pottery workshop in Patan and try your hand at making pottery. Continue to a thangka painting studio to observe the intricate process of creating these Buddhist scroll paintings. End the day exploring the ancient Asan market and Indrachowk area, experiencing the daily life of local Newars. Overnight in Kathmandu.

Day 5

Kathmandu Durbar Square & Free Day

Morning visit to Kathmandu Durbar Square, the historic royal palace complex featuring the Kumari Ghar (home of the living goddess), Taleju Temple, and numerous temples and statues. Explore the nearby markets for souvenirs. Afternoon free for additional sightseeing, shopping in Thamel, or relaxation. Optional visit to the Garden of Dreams or Pashupatinath for evening aarti ceremony. Farewell dinner at a traditional Newari restaurant. Overnight in Kathmandu.

Day 6

Departure

After breakfast, enjoy free time for last-minute shopping or sightseeing. Our representative will transfer you to Tribhuvan International Airport for your departure flight. Bid farewell to Nepal with memories of a lifetime!

Ancient Treasures

UNESCO World Heritage Sites

The Kathmandu Valley is home to seven UNESCO World Heritage Sites within a small area.

Kathmandu Durbar Square

The historic royal palace complex dating back to the 12th century. Features the Kumari Ghar (living goddess temple), Taleju Temple, and numerous statues and temples.

Patan Durbar Square

Known as the "City of Fine Arts," this square showcases exquisite Newari architecture, including the famous Krishna Temple with its golden roof.

Bhaktapur Durbar Square

The best-preserved ancient city with medieval architecture. Famous for the Nyatapola Temple, the tallest pagoda in Nepal, and the 55-Window Palace.

Swayambhunath

The "Monkey Temple" sits atop a hill overlooking Kathmandu Valley. One of the oldest Buddhist shrines in the world with 365 steps leading to the top.

Boudhanath Stupa

One of the largest spherical stupas in the world, dating to the 5th century. A major pilgrimage center for Buddhists with a rich Tibetan Buddhist community.

Pashupatinath Temple

The most sacred Hindu temple in Nepal, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Located on the banks of the Bagmati River with important cremation ghats.

Questions Answered

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about the Cultural Nepal Tour.

What UNESCO World Heritage Sites will I visit in Nepal?

You will visit several UNESCO World Heritage Sites including: Kathmandu Durbar Square (the ancient royal palace), Patan Durbar Square (the city of fine arts), Bhaktapur Durbar Square (the city of devotees), Swayambhunath (the Monkey Temple), Boudhanath Stupa (the great stupa), and Pashupatinath Temple (the holy temple of Lord Shiva). These sites represent the rich cultural and religious heritage of Nepal spanning over two millennia.

What is Newari culture and why is it significant?

Newari culture refers to the traditions and customs of the Newar people, the indigenous inhabitants of the Kathmandu Valley. Known for their exceptional artistry, the Newars have contributed immensely to Nepal's art, architecture, cuisine, and festivals. Their Newar Buddhist traditions are unique in the world. You'll experience Newari culture through their distinctive pagoda-style temples, intricate wood carvings, traditional pottery, and famous Newari cuisine.

How much walking is involved in this tour?

This tour involves moderate walking, typically 2-4 hours per day exploring heritage sites. The terrain in Kathmandu Valley is relatively flat, though there are some stairs at temple complexes. Comfortable walking shoes are essential. Our guides will ensure comfortable pacing with rest stops. The tour is suitable for most fitness levels, though some sites require climbing stairs and uneven surfaces.

What traditional experiences are included in the tour?

The tour includes several authentic cultural experiences: a traditional Newari cooking class where you learn to prepare local dishes, pottery making demonstration in Patan, visits to traditional workshops producing Buddhist statues and thankas (paintings), interaction with local families, traditional music and dance performances, and exploration of ancient markets.

What should I wear when visiting temples in Nepal?

When visiting temples and religious sites in Nepal, dress modestly and respectfully. Cover your shoulders and knees as a minimum. Remove your shoes before entering Hindu temples and Buddhist shrines. Avoid wearing shorts, tank tops, or revealing clothing. Head coverings are not required but may be appreciated at certain Buddhist sites.

Can this tour be customized to my preferences?

Absolutely! We offer fully customizable tours. You can extend or shorten the itinerary, add specific destinations, or focus on particular aspects of Nepali culture. Options include: adding a day trip to Nagarkot for sunrise mountain views, including yoga and meditation sessions, or including visits to rural villages for authentic homestay experiences.
