Trip Highlights

Discover the spiritual heart of Buddhism at these remarkable sacred sites in Lumbini.

🕎

Maya Devi Temple

Visit the exact spot where Queen Maya Devi gave birth to Siddhartha Gautama, who became Lord Buddha. The temple houses the Marker Stone marking the precise birthplace.

⛰

Ashoka Pillar

Behold the ancient Ashoka Pillar, erected by Emperor Ashoka in 249 BC, one of the oldest Buddhist monuments in the world with inscriptions in Brahmi script.

🌱

Sacred Garden

Explore the peaceful Sacred Garden featuring ancient ruins, archaeological excavations, and the legendary Lotus Pond where Maya Devi is said to have bathed.

🏭

Monastic Zones

Visit the beautiful monastic zones representing Buddhist traditions from Myanmar, Thailand, China, Japan, Tibet, and Vietnam, each with unique architecture.

🚇

Royal Bath

See the ancient Royal Bath (Rupandehi), a sacred pool where Queen Maya Devi rested after her difficult journey before giving birth to Buddha.

👀

World Peace Pagoda

Visit the magnificent World Peace Pagoda, a symbol of peace and harmony, offering panoramic views of the Lumbini landscape.

Your Spiritual Journey

Day-by-Day Itinerary

A carefully planned 3-4 day pilgrimage to ensure a meaningful and peaceful experience at Buddha's birthplace.

Day 1

Arrival in Lumbini

Arrive in Lumbini via flight from Kathmandu to Bhairahawa (Siddharthanagar). Our representative will greet you and transfer to your hotel. In the afternoon, visit the Maya Devi Temple, the most sacred site in Lumbini where Buddha was born. Explore the Sacred Garden with its ancient ruins and archaeological findings. Witness the Marker Stone that marks the exact birthplace. End the day with a peaceful evening walk around the serene grounds. Overnight in Lumbini.

Day 2

Full Day Lumbini Exploration

After breakfast, visit the iconic Ashoka Pillar, erected by Emperor Ashoka in 249 BC, one of the oldest Buddhist monuments with ancient inscriptions. Explore the Royal Bath (Rupandehi), where Maya Devi is believed to have bathed before giving birth. Visit the various Monastic Zones: the Myanmar Golden Temple with its stunning golden stupa, the Thai Royal Buddhist Temple, the Chinese Temple, the Japanese Temple, and the Tibetan monastery. Explore the Maya Devi Zoo and the Lumbini Museum to learn about the region's rich Buddhist heritage. Overnight in Lumbini.

Day 3

Buddhist Sites & Departure

Begin with an early morning visit to the World Peace Pagoda for sunrise views and meditation. After breakfast, optional visit to nearby Buddhist sites or the local market for souvenirs. Depending on your schedule, depart for Kathmandu via flight or continue to your next destination. Our representative will assist with all departure arrangements. If time permits, we can also arrange a visit to the Tilaurakot (ancient Kapilavastu), the childhood home of Prince Siddhartha, located approximately 27 km from Lumbini.

Day 4 (Optional)

Extended Exploration & Departure

This optional fourth day allows for deeper exploration. Visit the ancient city of Tilaurakot (Kapilavastu), the palace where Siddhartha Gautama spent his childhood years. Explore the Archaeological Museum and view excavated artifacts from ancient Buddhist structures. Visit the Gotihawa and Niglihawa Buddhist sites. Alternatively, enjoy a meditation session at one of the monasteries or simply relax in the peaceful surroundings before your departure.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit Lumbini

Understanding the seasons helps ensure a comfortable and meaningful pilgrimage.

☀ October - March

Best Season

Cool, dry weather with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 25°C during the day. Ideal for sightseeing and meditation. Clear skies provide pleasant conditions for exploring outdoor sites. This is peak pilgrimage season with comfortable walking conditions.

🌨 April - May

Warm Season

Temperatures can rise to 35°C or higher. Early morning and evening visits are recommended. Buddha Jayanti festival (usually in May) attracts thousands of pilgrims. The heat can be challenging but the spiritual atmosphere is exceptional.

🌧 June - September

Monsoon Season

Heavy monsoon rains can disrupt travel plans. Some roads may be difficult to navigate. However, the landscape is lush and green, and there are fewer tourists. Not recommended for most travelers.

Questions Answered

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about the Lumbini Pilgrimage Tour.

What makes Lumbini a significant pilgrimage destination?

Lumbini is one of the most sacred Buddhist pilgrimage sites in the world as it is the birthplace of Siddhartha Gautama, who later became Lord Buddha. UNESCO recognized Lumbini as a World Heritage Site in 1997. The site contains the exact spot where Maya Devi gave birth to Buddha, marked by the Maya Devi Temple. The Ashoka Pillar, erected by Emperor Ashoka in 249 BC, is one of the oldest Buddhist monuments in the world.

How do I get to Lumbini from Kathmandu?

Lumbini is accessible by both air and road from Kathmandu. The most convenient option is a 35-minute domestic flight to Bhairahawa (Siddharthanagar), followed by a 30-minute drive to Lumbini. Alternatively, you can take a tourist bus or private vehicle, which takes approximately 7-8 hours via the East-West Highway. We provide all transportation arrangements as part of the tour package.

What should I wear when visiting Lumbini?

As Lumbini is a sacred religious site, visitors should dress modestly and respectfully. Cover your shoulders and knees when visiting temples and monasteries. Loose, comfortable clothing in neutral colors is recommended. Remove your shoes before entering the Maya Devi Temple and other Buddhist shrines. During certain Buddhist festivals, white clothing is traditionally worn by pilgrims.

What are the main attractions in Lumbini?

The main attractions include: Maya Devi Temple (the exact birthplace), Ashoka Pillar (erected in 249 BC), Sacred Garden (ancient ruins and archaeology), Royal Bath (where Maya Devi bathed before giving birth), various Monastic Zones (Tibetan, Thai, Myanmar, Chinese, Japanese, and Vietnamese monasteries), the Lotus Pond, and the World Peace Pagoda. Each zone showcases unique Buddhist architecture from different countries.

Is Lumbini suitable for families and elderly travelers?

Absolutely! Lumbini is one of the easiest pilgrimage destinations in Nepal. The sites are relatively flat and accessible, making it suitable for elderly travelers and families with children. There are no high altitudes to worry about, and the weather is generally pleasant. The peaceful atmosphere makes it ideal for meditation and reflection. Our tours can be customized to include comfortable accommodations and a relaxed pace.

When is the best time to visit Lumbini?

The best time to visit Lumbini is from October to March when the weather is cool and dry. The monsoon season (June-September) brings heavy rains that can make sightseeing difficult. Winter nights can be cold (December-February), but days are pleasant. Buddhist festivals like Buddha Jayanti (Buddha's birthday, usually in May) attract large crowds and offer unique cultural experiences.
