Experience the Remote Beauty of Kanchanjunga

The Kanchanjunga Circuit Trek is one of Nepal's most spectacular and challenging adventures, circling around the magnificent Mount Kanchanjunga (8,586m), the world's third highest mountain. This extraordinary 24-day journey takes you through some of the most remote and pristine landscapes in the Himalayas, far from the crowds that flock to more famous trekking routes.

Located in the far eastern region of Nepal, the Kanchanjunga Circuit offers an unparalleled wilderness experience. Trek through dense rhododendron forests, cross high mountain passes, and visit traditional Buddhist villages where ancient traditions remain unchanged for centuries. The region is home to the endangered snow leopard, red panda, and Himalayan tahr, making it a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts.

This challenging but rewarding trek is perfect for experienced trekkers seeking a true adventure off the beaten path. With proper acclimatization and an experienced guide from Trekking Team Nepal, you'll conquer high altitudes and remote terrain to witness some of the most breathtaking mountain scenery on Earth.

Day by Day

Kanchanjunga Circuit Trek Itinerary

Day 1-2: Arrival and Fly to Birtapool

Kathmandu (1,300m) - Birtapool - Taplejung

Arrive in Kathmandu and receive a warm welcome with airport transfer. Enjoy a welcome dinner with traditional cultural show and detailed trek briefing. On Day 2, take a scenic flight from Kathmandu to Birtapool, then trek to Taplejung (1,850m), the starting point of our Kanchanjunga adventure.

Day 3-4: Trek to Kande

Kande (2,438m)

Continue trekking through terraced farmland and dense forests. Pass by small villages and prayer flags fluttering in the wind. Arrive at Kande, a peaceful settlement surrounded by mountains, perfect for acclimatization.

Day 5-6: Trek to Sinion La

Sinion La (4,440m) - Lelep

Ascend towards the Sinion La pass at 4,440m. The trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding peaks. Cross the pass and descend to Lelep, a small settlement in the valley, preparing for the main base camp approach.

Day 7-8: Trek to Pangpema (Kanchanjunga Base Camp)

Pangpema (5,160m)

The highlight of the trek - reach Pangpema, the Kanchanjunga Base Camp at 5,160m. Witness the spectacular north face of Kanchanjunga and its surrounding peaks. Spend time exploring the base camp area and capturing the majestic mountain scenery.

Day 9-12: Exploration and Return

Pangpema Area

Spend days exploring the base camp area, taking in the panoramic views of Kanchanjunga's massive peaks. Visit the Japanese Camp and explore the glacier. Begin the return journey, retracing steps through the beautiful landscapes.

Day 13-15: Cross Passes to Lhonak

Lhonak (4,780m)

Cross high mountain passes as we make our way to Lhonak. This remote valley offers stunning views of the eastern face of Kanchanjunga. Lhonak is a yak grazing area with basic teahouses, providing a true wilderness experience.

Day 16-18: Trek to Ghunsa

Ghunsa (3,595m)

Descend to the beautiful village of Ghunsa (3,595m). This traditional Tibetan Buddhist village is home to ancient monasteries and offers a glimpse into local culture. Explore the village, visit the centuries-old monastery, and interact with the friendly locals.

Day 19-21: Trek Back to Road Head

Tumlingtar

Trek through forests and villages towards the road head at Tumlingtar. The descent is easier but equally scenic. Arrive at Tumlingtar, where vehicles await to take you to the airport for your flight back to Kathmandu.

Day 22-24: Return to Kathmandu and Departure

Kathmandu (1,300m)

Fly back to Kathmandu from Tumlingtar. Enjoy buffer days in case of weather delays. Spend your last days exploring Kathmandu's cultural attractions, shopping for souvenirs, or simply relaxing. Depart with unforgettable memories of the Kanchanjunga Circuit Trek.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Kanchanjunga Circuit Trek?

The Kanchanjunga Circuit Trek is a 24-day guided trek that circles around Mount Kanchanjunga (8,586m), the world's third highest mountain. Located in Eastern Nepal, this trek takes you through remote wilderness, traditional Buddhist villages, and the stunning Pangpema (Kanchanjunga Base Camp) at 5,160m elevation.

How difficult is the Kanchanjunga Circuit Trek?

The Kanchanjunga Circuit Trek is rated as Extreme/Challenging. This is one of the most difficult treks in Nepal due to remote locations, high altitudes reaching 5,160m, multiple high passes, and long walking days. Prior high-altitude trekking experience and excellent physical fitness are strongly recommended.

When is the best time for Kanchanjunga Circuit Trek?

The best times to trek the Kanchanjunga Circuit are spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November). Spring offers rhododendron blooms and mild weather, while autumn provides clear skies and excellent mountain visibility. Both seasons avoid the monsoon rains and extreme winter cold.

What permits do I need for Kanchanjunga Circuit Trek?

The Kanchanjunga Circuit Trek requires several permits including the Kanchanjunga Conservation Area Permit (KCAP), Trekking Information Management System (TIMS) card, and a special restricted area permit. All permits are arranged by Trekking Team Nepal as part of the package.

What is the maximum altitude on the Kanchanjunga Circuit Trek?

The maximum altitude on the Kanchanjunga Circuit Trek is 5,160m at Pangpema (Kanchanjunga Base Camp). This is a high-altitude trek that requires proper acclimatization, and the itinerary includes rest days to help your body adjust to the thin air.

What wildlife can I see on the Kanchanjunga Circuit Trek?

The Kanchanjunga region is home to rare wildlife including the endangered snow leopard, red panda, Himalayan tahr, musk deer, and various species of pheasants. The Kanchanjunga Conservation Area protects this remarkable biodiversity, making wildlife sighting a highlight of the trek.
