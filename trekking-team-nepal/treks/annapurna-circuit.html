Experience Nepal's Most Iconic Trekking Route

The Annapurna Circuit trek is one of the world's most celebrated long-distance treks, offering an unparalleled Himalayan adventure through diverse landscapes, ancient villages, and breathtaking mountain scenery. This 19-day journey takes you around the entire Annapurna Massif, crossing the legendary Thorong La Pass at 5,416 meters above sea level.

Beginning in the subtropical lowlands of the Marsyangdi Valley, you'll gradually ascend through terraced rice fields, rhododendron forests, and alpine meadows before reaching the high-altitude desert landscape around Muktinath. The trek showcases the remarkable diversity of Nepal's geography and cultures, from Hindu temples to Buddhist monasteries, from lush green valleys to stark high-altitude terrain.

The highlight of the trek is undoubtedly crossing the Thorong La Pass, one of the highest trekking passes in the world, followed by the magical sunrise at Poon Hill overlooking the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri mountain ranges. This classic adventure combines physical challenge with cultural immersion, making it a bucket-list experience for outdoor enthusiasts and adventure travelers alike.

Detailed Itinerary

Your 19-Day Annapurna Circuit Adventure

A carefully planned route ensuring proper acclimatization and unforgettable experiences

1 Arrival in Kathmandu (1,300m)

+

Welcome to Nepal! Upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport, you will be greeted by our representative and transferred to your hotel in Thamel. In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner with a traditional Nepali cultural show. Our guide will provide a comprehensive trek briefing and check your equipment.

Overnight: Hotel in Kathmandu (3-star)

2 Drive to Besisahar (760m)

+

After an early breakfast, we begin our journey by road to Besisahar, the starting point of the Annapurna Circuit trek. The scenic 6-7 hour drive follows the Trishuli River valley, offering views of terraced hillsides and rural Nepal. Besisahar is the headquarters of Lamjung district and marks the beginning of our trek.

Overnight: Teahouse in Besisahar

Driving: 6-7 hours

3 Trek to Bahundanda (1,310m)

+

Our trek begins! Today we follow the Marsyangdi River upstream, passing through charming villages with stone houses, terraced rice fields, and lush vegetation. The trail undulates through subtropical forest, offering glimpses of local life. Bahundanda, meaning "Brahmin hill," sits atop a ridge with panoramic views of the surrounding hills.

Overnight: Teahouse in Bahundanda

Trekking: 6-7 hours

4 Trek to Chame (2,670m)

+

The landscape begins to change as we ascend higher. We trek through dense forests of oak and pine, with the sound of the Marsyangdi River accompanying us. As we approach Chame, the capital of the Manang district, we catch our first views of the towering peaks including the Annapurna II and Lamjung Himal.

Overnight: Teahouse in Chame

Trekking: 5-6 hours

5 Trek to Upper Pisang (3,300m)

+

Today's trek takes us through dramatic alpine scenery with dramatic rock formations and cliff-side trails. The vegetation becomes sparser as we gain altitude. Upper Pisang offers stunning views of the surrounding peaks and a more traditional Tibetan-influenced culture. The village is known for its apple orchards and ancient monastery.

Overnight: Teahouse in Upper Pisang

Trekking: 5-6 hours

6 Trek to Manang (3,540m) - Acclimatization Day

+

We continue our ascent to Manang, a beautiful village nestled at the foot of the Annapurna Range. This is our acclimatization stop, allowing our bodies to adjust to the high altitude. Manang has a rich Buddhist heritage with several monasteries and prayer flags fluttering in the wind. Explore the village and enjoy the stunning mountain backdrop.

Overnight: Teahouse in Manang

Trekking: 5-6 hours

7 Explore Manang - Hike to Gangapurna Lake

+

Rest day in Manang! After a relaxed morning, we take a scenic hike to Gangapurna Lake, a glacial lake at the base of Gangapurna peak. The hike offers spectacular views of the Annapurna III, Gangapurna, and Tilicho Peak. Alternatively, you can explore the Ice Lake or visit the ancient monastery in the village.

Overnight: Teahouse in Manang

Hiking: 3-4 hours

8 Trek to Yak Kharka (4,018m)

+

We begin our approach to the Thorong La Pass. The trail ascends gradually through alpine meadows and past yak grazing grounds. The landscape becomes more barren and rocky as we approach Yak Kharka, a small settlement at high altitude. Keep a steady pace and stay hydrated to aid acclimatization.

Overnight: Teahouse in Yak Kharka

Trekking: 4-5 hours

9 Cross Thorong La Pass (5,416m) to Muktinath (3,800m)

+

The most challenging and rewarding day! We wake early (around 4 AM) to cross the Thorong La Pass, one of the highest trekking passes in the world. The pre-dawn start ensures we reach the pass by sunrise, when the mountains are painted golden pink. After celebrating at the top, we descend to the sacred pilgrimage site of Muktinath, a Hindu and Buddhist temple complex.

Overnight: Teahouse in Muktinath

Trekking: 7-8 hours

10 Trek to Jomsom (2,720m)

+

Descending from the high altitude, we follow the Kali Gandaki River through the world's deepest gorge. Jomsom is the district headquarters of Mustang and a major hub in the Annapurna Circuit. The town is known for its apple orchards and the famous Jomsom Om Mane Padme Hum trek route.

Overnight: Teahouse in Jomsom

Trekking: 5-6 hours

11 Trek to Marpha (2,650m)

+

A short and pleasant walk takes us to the beautiful village of Marpha. Known as the "Apple Village," Marpha is famous for its apple orchards, apple brandy, and traditional whitewashed houses. The village has a rich Tibetan Buddhist heritage and offers stunning views of the Dhaulagiri range.

Overnight: Teahouse in Marpha

Trekking: 2-3 hours

12 Trek to Kalopani (2,530m)

+

We continue our journey through the Kali Gandaki valley, one of the world's deepest river gorges. The trail offers spectacular views of Dhaulagiri (8,167m), the seventh highest mountain in the world. Kalopani is situated at the confluence of the Kali Gandaki and its tributary, offering breathtaking mountain scenery.

Overnight: Teahouse in Kalopani

Trekking: 5-6 hours

13 Trek to Tatopani (1,190m) - Hot Springs

+

Today we descend to Tatopani, which means "hot water" in Nepali. After days of high-altitude trekking, there's nothing better than relaxing in the natural hot springs. The geothermal springs are believed to have healing properties and are the perfect way to soothe tired muscles. The climate changes dramatically as we enter the subtropical lowlands.

Overnight: Teahouse in Tatopani

Trekking: 5-6 hours

14 Trek to Ghorepani (2,850m)

+

We begin our ascent to Ghorepani, passing through dense rhododendron forests. In spring, the trail is ablaze with colorful rhododendron blooms - Nepal's national flower. Ghorepani is a small village that serves as a base for the famous Poon Hill sunrise viewpoint. The village has excellent views of the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges.

Overnight: Teahouse in Ghorepani

Trekking: 6-7 hours

15 Sunrise at Poon Hill - Trek to Tikhedunga - Drive to Pokhara

+

Wake up early for the iconic Poon Hill sunrise! The 45-minute hike to the viewpoint rewards you with one of the most spectacular mountain panoramas in the world. Watch as the first rays of sunlight paint the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri peaks in shades of gold and pink. After breakfast, we trek down to Tikhedunga and then drive to the lakeside city of Pokhara for a well-deserved rest.

Overnight: Hotel in Pokhara (3-star)

Trekking: 3-4 hours, Driving: 1-2 hours

16 Drive back to Kathmandu

+

We drive back to Kathmandu, enjoying the scenic route along the Trishuli River. The rest of the day is free for shopping, exploring Thamel market, or simply relaxing at the hotel. In the evening, enjoy a farewell dinner with your trekking team to celebrate your achievement.

Overnight: Hotel in Kathmandu (3-star)

Driving: 6-7 hours

17-18 Buffer Days

+

These days are reserved as contingency in case of unexpected delays due to weather, health issues, or other circumstances. If everything goes according to plan, these days can be used to explore Kathmandu's cultural heritage sites, go shopping for souvenirs, or enjoy additional activities in the city.

Overnight: Hotel in Kathmandu (3-star)

19 Departure

+

Your amazing Annapurna Circuit adventure comes to an end. Our representative will transfer you to Tribhuvan International Airport for your departure flight. We hope you leave with unforgettable memories of the Himalayas and look forward to welcoming you back for future adventures in Nepal!

Meals: Breakfast

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the Annapurna Circuit Trek

How difficult is the Annapurna Circuit trek?

+

The Annapurna Circuit is rated as moderate to challenging. While no technical climbing experience is required, you should have a good fitness level and be prepared for 5-8 hours of walking daily. The most challenging day is crossing the Thorong La Pass at 5,416m, which requires early morning starts and determination. The trail includes steep ascents and descents, and you'll experience significant altitude changes. We recommend preparing with regular cardiovascular exercise for at least 2-3 months before your trek. Previous trekking experience is helpful but not mandatory for fit and determined beginners.

Do I need prior trekking experience for Annapurna Circuit?

+

Prior trekking experience is beneficial but not strictly required for the Annapurna Circuit. What's most important is having good physical fitness, mental preparation, and willingness to embrace the challenge. The trek is achievable for committed beginners who prepare adequately. If you're new to high-altitude trekking, we recommend starting with shorter treks like the Ghorepani Poon Hill trek to build your experience. Our experienced guides will provide support and guidance throughout the journey, ensuring your safety and enjoyment.

Is altitude sickness a concern on the Annapurna Circuit?

+

Altitude sickness is a genuine concern on the Annapurna Circuit, particularly when crossing the Thorong La Pass at 5,416m. However, with proper acclimatization, most trekkers adjust well to the altitude. Our itinerary includes a dedicated acclimatization day in Manang (3,540m). Symptoms of mild altitude sickness include headache, nausea, fatigue, and loss of appetite. Our guides are trained to recognize signs of AMS and will advise on appropriate measures. To minimize risk, walk slowly, stay hydrated, avoid alcohol, and inform your guide immediately if you feel unwell.

When is the best time to trek the Annapurna Circuit?

+

The best times to trek the Annapurna Circuit are spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). Spring offers warmer temperatures, clear skies, and famous rhododendron blooms. Autumn provides stable weather, excellent visibility, and the festive atmosphere of Dashain and Tihar. Winter (December to February) is extremely cold with heavy snowfall above 3,000m. Summer/monsoon (June to August) brings heavy rains and poor visibility.

What should I pack for the Annapurna Circuit trek?

+

Essential items include: layered clothing (moisture-wicking base layers, insulating mid-layers, waterproof outer shell), comfortable broken-in trekking boots, warm down jacket, gloves, warm hat, and sunglasses with UV protection. Bring a good quality sleeping bag (rated to -10°C or colder), trekking poles, and a daypack (30-40L). Other essentials: water bottles, water purification tablets, sunscreen, lip balm, first-aid kit, headlamp, and quick-dry towels. Pack light - you'll only need about 8-10kg in your daypack as porters carry your main luggage.

What is the accommodation like on the Annapurna Circuit?

+

During the trek, you'll stay in teahouses - simple mountain lodges offering basic but comfortable rooms with two beds, blankets, and pillows. Common areas have dining spaces where meals are served. In Kathmandu and Pokhara, you'll stay in 3-star hotels with comfortable amenities. Hot showers are available at most teahouses but may cost extra. WiFi is available in some teahouses but can be unreliable. The variety and quality of teahouses has improved significantly in recent years.
