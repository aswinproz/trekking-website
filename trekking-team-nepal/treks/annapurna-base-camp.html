Journey to the Heart of the Himalayas

The Annapurna Base Camp trek, also known as the ABC Trek, is one of Nepal's most popular and rewarding treks. This 14-day adventure takes you deep into the Annapurna Sanctuary, surrounded by some of the world's highest peaks including Annapurna I (8,091m), Annapurna South (7,219m), and the iconic fishtail peak of Machapuchare (6,993m).

The trek begins with a scenic drive from Kathmandu to Pokhara, followed by a journey through traditional Gurung villages, dense rhododendron forests, and alpine terrain. One of the highlights is the breathtaking sunrise at Poon Hill, where you can witness the entire Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges illuminate in golden hues.

Unlike the Annapurna Circuit, the ABC trek follows a more direct route into the heart of the mountains, offering a more intimate experience with the Himalayas. The trek culminates at 4,130 meters, where you stand at the base of these magnificent peaks, an experience that will stay with you forever.

Trek Highlights

  • Spectacular sunrise views at Poon Hill over Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges
  • Entry into the sacred Annapurna Sanctuary at 4,130m
  • Traditional Gurung villages including Chhomrong and Ghandruk
  • Relaxing natural hot springs at Jhinu Danda
  • Diverse flora and fauna in rhododendron and bamboo forests
  • Up-close views of Annapurna I, Annapurna South, and Machapuchare

Detailed Itinerary

1 Arrival in Kathmandu

+

Welcome to Nepal! Upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport, you will be greeted by our representative and transferred to your hotel. In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner with a traditional Nepali cultural show. Later, attend a trek briefing where your guide will provide details about the journey ahead and check your trekking gear.

Overnight: Kathmandu (Hotel)

2 Drive to Pokhara (820m)

+

After an early breakfast, embark on a scenic 6-7 hour drive to Pokhara, Nepal's lake city. The drive offers breathtaking views of countryside villages, terraced rice fields, and mountain landscapes. Upon arrival in Pokhara, check into your hotel and spend the evening strolling along the serene Phewa Lake. Prepare your gear for the trek the next day.

Overnight: Pokhara (Hotel)

3 Drive to Nayapul (1,070m) - Trek to Tikhedunga (1,540m)

+

After breakfast, drive for about 1 hour to Nayapul, the starting point of our trek. Begin your journey through charming villages and terraced rice fields. The trail follows the Modi Khola river, passing through Birethanti, a small town with shops and teahouses. Continue to Tikhedunga, where you'll spend the night. This is a relatively easy day to help with acclimatization.

Trekking: 4-5 hours | Overnight: Tikhedunga (Teahouse)

4 Trek to Ghorepani (2,850m)

+

Today is a challenging day as you ascend steep stone steps to reach Ghorepani. The trail passes through dense forests of rhododendron and oak, which are particularly beautiful in spring when the rhododendrons are in bloom. Ghorepani is a large village known for its strategic position for the next day's sunrise view. Relax and prepare for the early morning hike.

Trekking: 5-6 hours | Overnight: Ghorepani (Teahouse)

5 Sunrise at Poon Hill - Trek to Tadapani (2,630m)

+

Wake up before dawn for the highlight of the trek - the Poon Hill sunrise. Hike 45 minutes to the viewpoint in darkness, and wait as the first rays of sunlight transform the snow-capped peaks into gold. The 360-degree view includes Annapurna I, Annapurna South, Dhaulagiri, Machapuchare, and many other peaks. Return for breakfast, then descend through forests to Tadapani.

Trekking: 5-6 hours | Overnight: Tadapani (Teahouse)

6 Trek to Chhomrong (2,170m)

+

Today's trek descends through forests to the Kimrong Khola, then ascends to Chhomrong, the largest Gurung village in the Annapurna region. The village offers excellent views of Annapurna South and Hiunchuli. Explore the traditional stone houses, visit the local monastery, and interact with the friendly Gurung people. This is your gateway to the Annapurna Sanctuary.

Trekking: 5-6 hours | Overnight: Chhomrong (Teahouse)

7 Trek to Bamboo (2,310m)

+

Descend from Chhomrong to the Chhomrong Khola, then ascend through bamboo and rhododendron forests. The trail becomes progressively quieter as you enter the Annapurna Conservation Area. Listen for the calls of various bird species and keep an eye out for wildlife. Bamboo gets its name from the extensive bamboo groves in the area.

Trekking: 5-6 hours | Overnight: Bamboo (Teahouse)

8 Trek to Deurali (3,230m)

+

The vegetation changes from bamboo to alpine shrubs as you ascend higher. The trail passes through a beautiful valley and becomes steeper. You'll notice the air getting thinner and the temperature dropping. Deurali offers stunning views and is a great place to acclimatize before reaching base camp.

Trekking: 4-5 hours | Overnight: Deurali (Teahouse)

9 Trek to Annapurna Base Camp (4,130m)

+

The final push to Annapurna Base Camp! The trail ascends through the Himalayan mega-biodiversity corridor. As you emerge from the forest, the valley opens up to reveal the spectacular amphitheater of peaks. Stand at the base of Annapurna I, Annapurna South, Hiunchuli, and the iconic Machapuchare (Fishtail). Watch the sunset paint the mountains in shades of pink and gold.

Trekking: 5-6 hours | Overnight: ABC (Teahouse)

10 Explore ABC - Trek back to Bamboo

+

Wake up early for another spectacular sunrise at base camp - it's worth seeing twice! Spend the morning exploring the area, taking photographs, and absorbing the incredible mountain scenery. The shrine at the entrance to the sanctuary is a sacred site. After lunch, begin the descent back through Deurali to Bamboo, enjoying the different perspective of the trail.

Trekking: 6-7 hours | Overnight: Bamboo (Teahouse)

11 Trek to Jhinu Danda (1,780m) - Natural Hot Springs

+

Descend from Bamboo back to Chhomrong, then continue to Jhinu Danda. This is a well-deserved rest day! In the afternoon, take a short walk to the natural hot springs beside the river. Soak in the warm therapeutic waters - a perfect way to relax tired muscles after days of trekking. The hot springs are believed to have healing properties.

Trekking: 5-6 hours | Overnight: Jhinu Danda (Teahouse)

12 Trek to Nayapul - Drive to Pokhara

+

Continue descending through villages and terraced fields to Nayapul. The trail is easier now as you return to lower altitudes. Upon reaching Nayapul, board the vehicle for the drive back to Pokhara. The evening is free to explore the lakeside area, enjoy a dinner by the lake, or take a boat ride on Phewa Lake.

Trekking: 5-6 hours | Drive: 1.5 hours | Overnight: Pokhara (Hotel)

13 Drive back to Kathmandu

+

After breakfast, drive back to Kathmandu along the same scenic route. The rest of the afternoon is free for shopping in Thamel, visiting temples, or simply relaxing at the hotel. In the evening, enjoy a farewell dinner to celebrate the completion of your incredible Himalayan adventure.

Drive: 6-7 hours | Overnight: Kathmandu (Hotel)

14 Departure

+

Depending on your flight schedule, you may have some free time for last-minute sightseeing or shopping. Our representative will transfer you to Tribhuvan International Airport for your departure flight. Depart Nepal with unforgettable memories of the Annapurna Base Camp trek and the warm hospitality of the Nepali people.

Departure: Airport transfer included
