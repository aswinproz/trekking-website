Why Choose Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek?

The Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek is one of the most popular and rewarding short treks in Nepal. This 10-day adventure takes you to Poon Hill (3,210m), famous for the best sunrise views in the entire Himalayas. Witness the golden light illuminate the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri mountain ranges in a breathtaking display of nature's grandeur.

Trip Highlights

  • Witness the world-famous Poon Hill sunrise over the Annapurna Range
  • Experience 360-degree panoramic views of Annapurna I, II, III, IV and Dhaulagiri
  • Trek through pristine rhododendron forests, especially beautiful in spring
  • Explore traditional Gurung villages including Ghandruk
  • Perfect introduction to Himalayan trekking - no prior experience needed
  • Visit the charming lakeside city of Pokhara

Day by Day

Complete Itinerary

10 days of adventure, culture, and the most spectacular sunrise views in the Himalayas

1 Arrival in Kathmandu

+

Upon arrival at Kathmandu International Airport, you will be warmly welcomed by our representative and transferred to your hotel. In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner with a traditional Nepali cultural show. Your trekking guide will provide a detailed trek briefing and help you check your gear. Overnight in Kathmandu.

2 Drive to Pokhara

+

After an early breakfast, embark on a scenic 6-7 hour drive from Kathmandu to Pokhara (827m). The journey takes you along the Trishuli River valley with beautiful mountain landscapes. Upon arrival in Pokhara, check into your hotel and enjoy an evening walk around the charming Phewa Lake. Overnight in Pokhara.

3 Drive to Nayapul - Trek to Tikhedunga

+

After breakfast, drive 1.5 hours to Nayapul (1,070m) where the real adventure begins. Start trekking to Tikhedunga (1,540m) through terraced fields and small villages. The trail follows the Modi Khola river valley. Today's trek is relatively easy, taking 4-5 hours. Overnight in Tikhedunga.

Trekking: 4-5 hours | 1,540m

4 Trek to Ghorepani

+

Trek from Tikhedunga to Ghorepani (2,850m). The trail ascends through dense rhododendron and oak forests. Pass by a beautiful waterfall and climb thousands of stone steps (over 3,000!) that lead to Ghorepani. The forest is especially beautiful during spring when rhododendrons are in bloom. Today's trek takes 6-7 hours. Overnight in Ghorepani.

Trekking: 6-7 hours | 2,850m

5 Sunrise at Poon Hill - Trek to Tadapani

+

Wake up early (around 4:30 AM) for the highlight of the trek - the famous Poon Hill sunrise! Hike 45 minutes in the dark to Poon Hill (3,210m). As the sun rises, the entire Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges are illuminated in golden light - a sight you'll never forget. Views include Annapurna I (8,091m), Annapurna II, III, IV, Dhaulagiri, and Machhapuchhre (Fish Tail).

Return to Ghorepani for breakfast, then continue trekking to Tadapani (2,630m). The trail descends through forest with occasional views of the mountains. Trekking time is 5-6 hours. Overnight in Tadapani.

Trekking: 5-6 hours | 3,210m (Poon Hill)

6 Trek to Ghandruk

+

Trek from Tadapani to Ghandruk (1,940m), one of the largest Gurung villages in the Annapurna region. Ghandruk is known for its traditional stone houses, friendly locals, and spectacular views of Machhapuchhre (Fish Tail Mountain). Visit the local Gurung museum to learn about the Gurung culture and history. Today's trek takes 4-5 hours. Overnight in Ghandruk.

Trekking: 4-5 hours | 1,940m

7 Trek to Kimrung Khola - Tolka - Drive to Pokhara

+

Trek from Ghandruk down to Kimrung Khola, then continue to Tolka. The trail descends through forests and terraced fields. From Tolka, drive back to Pokhara (1.5 hours). Enjoy a relaxing evening by the lake, perhaps taking a boat ride on Phewa Lake or exploring the Lakeside area. Overnight in Pokhara.

Trekking: 5-6 hours | Drive: 1.5 hours

8 Drive back to Kathmandu

+

After breakfast, drive back to Kathmandu (6-7 hours). The rest of the day is free for shopping in Thamel, exploring the city, or simply relaxing. In the evening, enjoy a farewell dinner with your trekking team. Overnight in Kathmandu.

Drive: 6-7 hours

9 Buffer Day - Kathmandu Exploration

+

This buffer day is included for acclimatization or unexpected delays due to weather or other circumstances. Use this day to explore Kathmandu's UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Visit the sacred Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple), Pashupatinath Temple, or the ancient city of Bhaktapur. Alternatively, relax and prepare for your departure. Overnight in Kathmandu.

10 Departure

+

After breakfast, transfer to Kathmandu International Airport for your departure flight. Your wonderful Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek adventure ends here with memories that will last a lifetime. We look forward to welcoming you back for another Himalayan adventure!

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek

How difficult is the Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek?

The Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek is classified as Easy difficulty, making it perfect for beginners and families. While you should have reasonable fitness level, no prior trekking experience is required. The highest point is Poon Hill at 3,210m, which is relatively low compared to other Nepal treks. The trail consists of mostly stone stairs and well-maintained paths. Daily walking is 4-7 hours on most days.

What is the best time to do the Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek?

The best time for Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). Spring offers rhododendron blooms in full color (especially April) and mild temperatures. Autumn provides clear skies, excellent visibility for sunrise views, and comfortable walking conditions. Winter (December to February) can be cold at higher altitudes with possible snow, while monsoon (June to August) brings heavy rainfall and leeches.

What permits do I need for the Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek?

For the Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek, you need two main permits: the Annapurna Conservation Area Permit (ACAP) and the Trekkers' Information Management System (TIMS) card. Both permits are included in your package with Trekking Team Nepal. Your guide will handle all permit arrangements before the trek begins, so you don't need to arrange anything yourself.

Will I experience altitude sickness on the Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek?

The risk of altitude sickness on the Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek is relatively low since the highest point is only 3,210m. However, it's still important to ascend slowly, stay hydrated, and inform your guide of any symptoms like headache or nausea. The gradual ascent and proper acclimatization built into the itinerary help minimize risks. Our experienced guides are trained to recognize and handle altitude-related issues.

What is the accommodation like during the Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek?

During the Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek, you'll stay in teahouses/lodges which offer basic but comfortable accommodation. Rooms are typically twin-share with warm blankets and pillows. Bathrooms are usually shared and can be either Western or Asian style, depending on the teahouse. In Ghorepani and popular villages, some teahouses offer attached bathrooms. All meals (breakfast, lunch, and dinner) are provided and prepared fresh at teahouses along the trail.

Is Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek suitable for beginners?

Yes, absolutely! Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek is one of the most beginner-friendly treks in Nepal. It's often recommended as a first trek for those new to Himalayan trekking. The trail is well-maintained, the ascent is gradual, and the 10-day itinerary allows for proper pacing. You'll need reasonable fitness and comfort with daily walking of 4-7 hours, but no prior trekking experience is necessary. The reward is the world-famous sunrise views from Poon Hill.
