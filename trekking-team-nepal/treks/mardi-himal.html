About This Trek

The Mardi Himal Trek is one of Nepal's best-kept secrets, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural immersion, and adventure. Often referred to as the "Hidden Gem of Annapurna," this trek takes you to the base of the majestic Machhapuchhre (Fish Tail Mountain), which stands at 6,993 meters and is considered sacred in Hindu mythology.

Unlike the more popular Annapurna Circuit and Base Camp treks, Mardi Himal remains off the beaten path, providing a more serene and intimate experience with nature. The trail winds through dense rhododendron forests, alpine meadows, and traditional Gurung villages, where you can experience the warm hospitality of the local people.

This 15-day itinerary is carefully designed for proper acclimatization, making it suitable for both beginners and experienced trekkers. The highest point of the trek is Mardi Himal Base Camp at 4,500 meters, where you'll be rewarded with breathtaking panoramic views of the Annapurna range, including Annapurna I, Annapurna South, and the iconic Fish Tail.

Detailed Itinerary

1 Arrival in Kathmandu

+

Welcome to Nepal! Upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport, our representative will greet you and transfer you to your hotel. In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner with a traditional cultural show. Later, attend a trek briefing and gear check to ensure you're fully prepared for the adventure ahead.

2 Drive to Pokhara

+

After an early breakfast, embark on a scenic 6-7 hour drive to Pokhara, Nepal's adventure capital. The journey passes through beautiful landscapes, terraced fields, and small villages. Upon arrival, check into your hotel and enjoy a leisurely evening walk along the scenic Phewa Lake. Prepare your gear for the trek tomorrow.

3 Trek to Australian Camp (2,060m)

+

Start your trekking adventure! Drive to the trailhead and begin your ascent through villages and forests to Australian Camp. Enjoy stunning views of the Annapurna range as you trek. The trail offers a gentle introduction to Himalayan trekking. Arrive at Australian Camp in the late afternoon and settle into your teahouse.

Trekking Duration: 4-5 hours

4 Trek to Forest Camp (2,520m)

+

Continue your journey through dense rhododendron and oak forests. Keep an eye out for wildlife including various bird species and possibly monkeys. The forest trail is serene and peaceful, especially away from the more crowded trails. Arrive at Forest Camp (also known as Pothana) in the afternoon.

Trekking Duration: 5-6 hours

5 Trek to High Camp (3,550m)

+

Today marks a significant altitude gain as you ascend through alpine terrain. The vegetation changes from dense forest to shrubs and eventually to alpine meadows. The views become increasingly spectacular, with Machhapuchhre (Fish Tail) becoming more prominent. Arrive at High Camp and enjoy the breathtaking sunset views.

Trekking Duration: 5-6 hours

6 Hike to Mardi Himal Base Camp (4,500m) - Return to High Camp

+

Wake up early for the highlight of the trek - the hike to Mardi Himal Base Camp at 4,500m. Watch the spectacular sunrise illuminate the peaks, creating a magical golden glow over Machhapuchhre and the Annapurna range. Spend time at the base camp taking in the incredible panoramic views before descending back to High Camp for the night.

Trekking Duration: 5-6 hours

7 Trek to Sidding (1,800m)

+

Begin your descent through rhododendron forests to Sidding village. The trail offers a different perspective of the landscape as you wind your way down. Arrive in Sidding, a small village with traditional houses and friendly locals. This is a great opportunity to experience local life.

Trekking Duration: 5-6 hours

8 Trek to Ghandruk (1,940m) - Gurung village

+

Trek to Ghandruk, one of the largest and most beautiful Gurung villages in the region. Explore the traditional stone houses, visit the Gurung Museum to learn about the local culture and traditions, and interact with the friendly Gurung people. Ghandruk offers stunning views of Annapurna South and Machhapuchhre.

Trekking Duration: 4-5 hours

9 Trek to Nayapul - Drive to Pokhara

+

Complete your final day of trekking as you descend to Nayapul. The trail passes through terraced fields and small villages. From Nayapul, drive back to Pokhara. Spend the evening relaxing by the lake, celebrating the completion of your Mardi Himal adventure.

Trekking Duration: 5-6 hours | Driving: 1 hour

10 Drive back to Kathmandu

+

Return to Kathmandu by scenic drive. The rest of the day is free for shopping in Thamel, exploring the city, or simply relaxing. In the evening, enjoy a farewell dinner to celebrate your successful trek.

Driving Duration: 6-7 hours

11-15 Buffer days and departure

+

These buffer days are included in case of weather delays or for additional acclimatization. Use this time to explore Kathmandu's cultural landmarks including Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple), Pashupatinath Temple, and the historic Durbar Square. On your departure day, our representative will transfer you to the airport for your onward journey.

Frequently Asked Questions

How difficult is the Mardi Himal Trek?

+

The Mardi Himal Trek is classified as Easy to Moderate difficulty. While no prior trekking experience is required, you should be physically fit and prepared for 5-7 hours of walking daily. The highest point reaches 4,500m, so altitude acclimatization is important. The trail is well-maintained with gradual ascents, making it suitable for beginners with reasonable fitness levels.

What is the best time to do the Mardi Himal Trek?

+

The best time for Mardi Himal Trek is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). Spring offers rhododendron blooms in full color and mild temperatures. Autumn provides clear skies, excellent visibility, and comfortable walking conditions. Monsoon season (June to August) brings heavy rainfall, while winter (December to February) can be extremely cold with snow at higher altitudes.

What permits do I need for Mardi Himal Trek?

+

For the Mardi Himal Trek, you need two main permits: the Annapurna Conservation Area Permit (ACAP) and the Trekkers' Information Management System (TIMS) card. Both permits are included in your package with Trekking Team Nepal. Your guide will handle all permit arrangements before the trek begins.

How high is Mardi Himal Base Camp and will I have altitude sickness?

+

Mardi Himal Base Camp sits at 4,500 meters (14,764 feet). While this is lower than Everest Base Camp, altitude sickness can still affect some trekkers. We include proper acclimatization days in our itinerary and recommend ascending slowly, staying hydrated, and informing your guide of any symptoms. Our guides are trained to recognize and handle altitude-related issues.

What is the accommodation like during the Mardi Himal Trek?

+

During the Mardi Himal Trek, you'll stay in teahouses/lodges which offer basic but comfortable accommodation. Rooms are typically twin-share with warm blankets. Bathrooms may be shared and can be either Western or Asian style. In higher altitudes, facilities are more basic. All meals are provided and prepared fresh at teahouses along the trail.

Is the Mardi Himal Trek suitable for beginners?

+

Yes, the Mardi Himal Trek is excellent for beginners! It's often called the "Hidden Gem of Annapurna" because it remains less crowded than other treks while offering equally stunning views. The trail is well-defined, gradual ascents make it manageable, and the 15-day duration allows proper acclimatization. No prior trekking experience is required, but basic fitness is recommended.
