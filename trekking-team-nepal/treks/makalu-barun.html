Experience the Untouched Wilderness of Makalu Barun

The Makalu Barun Trek offers an extraordinary adventure to one of Nepal's most remote and spectacular regions. Mount Makalu, standing at 8,485 meters, is the world's fifth highest mountain, and this trek provides unparalleled views of its massive summit while exploring the pristine Barun Valley.

Makalu Barun National Park is a biodiversity hotspot, protecting the eastern Himalayan ecosystem with its rich flora and fauna. This trek combines challenging high-altitude adventure with the opportunity to encounter rare wildlife including red pandas, snow leopards, and Himalayan black bears in their natural habitat.

Unlike more commercialized trekking routes, the Makalu Barun trail remains uncrowded, offering a truly authentic wilderness experience. Trek through ancient rhododendron forests, cross glacial rivers, and camp in remote locations where the only sounds are mountain streams and wildlife.

Trip Highlights

  • Fifth highest mountain - Mount Makalu (8,485m) base camp experience
  • Pristine Barun Valley - one of Nepal's least disturbed wilderness areas
  • Rich wildlife - red pandas, snow leopards, Himalayan tahr, and diverse bird species
  • Remote adventure - far from crowds, authentic wilderness experience
  • Dramatic landscapes - glacial valleys, waterfalls, and alpine meadows
  • Cultural encounters - traditional Rai and Sherpa villages
21-Day Adventure

Detailed Itinerary

A carefully planned route ensuring proper acclimatization and unforgettable experiences

1 Arrival in Kathmandu (1,300m)

+

Arrive at Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu. Our representative will greet you and transfer you to your hotel. In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner with a traditional Nepali cultural show. Receive a comprehensive trek briefing and check your equipment. Overnight in Kathmandu.

2 Fly to Tumlingtar & Trek to Seduwa (1,500m)

+

Take an early morning flight from Kathmandu to Tumlingtar (approximately 45 minutes). Upon arrival, begin your trek to Seduwa. The trail descends through terraced farmland and subtropical forest. Seduwa is a small village serving as the entrance to Makalu Barun National Park. Overnight in Seduwa teahouse.

3-4 Trek to Sherson (2,800m)

+

Ascend through dense rhododendron and bamboo forests. The trail follows the Arun River, passing through several small villages. As you gain altitude, the vegetation changes from subtropical to temperate. Sherson offers stunning views of the surrounding peaks and is a great location for wildlife spotting, especially during early morning. Overnight in Sherson.

5-6 Trek to Makalu Base Camp (5,000m)

+

The trail becomes increasingly rugged as you ascend through alpine terrain. Pass through the yak grazing areas and cross several glacial streams. The final approach to Makalu Base Camp offers spectacular close-up views of Mount Makalu and its massive west face. Set up camp near the base camp and spend time exploring the area. Overnight in tents at Base Camp.

7-8 Exploration and Acclimatization

+

Spend these days exploring the base camp area and surrounding glacial valleys. Take short hikes to higher viewpoints for better perspectives of Makalu and the surrounding peaks including Chamlang and Peak 6. These days are crucial for acclimatization. Enjoy the pristine mountain environment and search for wildlife in the rocky terrain. Return to Base Camp for overnight.

9-10 Return from Base Camp

+

Begin the descent back through the Barun Valley. The return journey offers a different perspective of the landscape. Depending on the route, you may pass through Sherpani Col approach or other scenic valleys. Continue to lower altitudes for better sleeping conditions. Overnight in teahouses along the trail.

11-15 Barun Valley Trek

+

Explore the pristine Barun Valley, a UNESCO World Heritage site component. The valley features dramatic waterfalls, glacial lakes, and diverse ecosystems. Trek through alpine meadows carpeted with wildflowers during spring. Keep watch for Himalayan tahr, musk deer, and colorful pheasants. The Barun River cuts through the valley, creating numerous small waterfalls. These days offer some of the best wilderness experiences in Nepal.

16-18 Return to Tumlingtar

+

Continue descending through the Barun Valley towards Tumlingtar. Pass through Mumbuk and other settlements. The trail follows the Arun River, descending through various vegetation zones. Arrive in Tumlingtar and prepare for your return flight to Kathmandu. Overnight in Tumlingtar.

19-20 Fly to Kathmandu

+

Morning flight from Tumlingtar back to Kathmandu. The rest of the day is free for shopping, sightseeing, or relaxation. Visit the famous Thamel market for trekking gear and souvenirs. In the evening, enjoy a farewell dinner. Overnight in Kathmandu.

21 Departure

+

After breakfast, transfer to Tribhuvan International Airport for your departure flight. Our representative will assist you with check-in and bid you farewell. End of Makalu Barun Trek adventure with memories of a lifetime.
