Your Journey

Day-by-Day Itinerary

A carefully planned 21-day adventure to ensure proper acclimatization and the best experience.

Day 1

Arrival in Kathmandu (1,300m)

Arrive at Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu. Our representative will greet you and transfer you to your hotel. In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner with a traditional Nepali cultural show. Overnight in Kathmandu.

Day 2

Kathmandu Sightseeing

Explore the UNESCO World Heritage Sites of Kathmandu including Pashupatinath Temple (Hindu holy site), Boudhanath Stupa (Buddhist pilgrimage center), Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple), and Kathmandu Durbar Square (ancient royal palace). Overnight in Kathmandu.

Day 3

Fly to Lukla (2,840m) & Trek to Phakding (2,610m)

Take an early morning flight from Kathmandu to Lukla (the world's most dramatic airport). After meeting our trekking crew, begin your journey with a 3-hour trek to Phakding. Enjoy your first night in a mountain teahouse. Trekking time: 3 hours.

Day 4

Trek to Namche Bazaar (3,440m)

Today's trek takes you through beautiful pine forests and across the Dudh Koshi River. The trail climbs steeply to the entrance of Sagarmatha National Park, then on to Namche Bazaar - the Sherpa capital. Get your first glimpse of Everest! Trekking time: 6 hours.

Day 5

Acclimatization Day

Rest day for acclimatization. Hike to the Everest View Hotel for spectacular views of Everest, Lhotse, and Ama Dablam. Explore Namche Bazaar, visit the Sherpa Museum, and enjoy the vibrant market. This is crucial for adjusting to the altitude.

Day 6

Trek to Tengboche (3,860m)

Descend to the Dudh Koshi River and climb through rhododendron forest to Tengboche. Visit the famous Tengboche Monastery, the largest Buddhist monastery in the Khumbu region. If you're lucky, you may witness the afternoon prayer ceremony. Trekking time: 5 hours.

Day 7

Trek to Dingboche (4,410m)

Enjoy spectacular views of Ama Dablam as you descend to the Imja River and climb to Dingboche. The landscape becomes more alpine as you gain altitude. Dingboche is known as the "Summer Valley" and offers stunning mountain panoramas. Trekking time: 5 hours.

Day 8

Acclimatization Day

Another crucial acclimatization day. Take a hike to Chhukung Valley or climb the small hill behind Dingboche for panoramic views of Island Peak, Makalu, and the Imja Tse glacier. Rest and prepare for the highest points ahead.

Day 9

Trek to Lobuche (4,940m)

Follow the Khumbu Glacier to Lobuche, a small settlement at the foot of the mighty Lobuche Peak. The trail becomes increasingly rugged as you approach the Everest region. Marvel at the views of the Khumbu Ice Fall. Trekking time: 5 hours.

Day 10

Trek to Everest Base Camp (5,364m) & Return to Gorakshep

The big day! Trek from Lobuche to Gorakshep, then continue to Everest Base Camp. Stand at the foot of the world's highest mountain and see the famous Khumbu Ice Fall. After exploring the base camp, return to Gorakshep for the night. Trekking time: 8 hours.

Day 11

Sunrise at Kalapathar (5,545m) & Trek to Pheriche

Wake up early for the famous Kalapathar sunrise. Watch the golden light illuminate Everest and the surrounding peaks - a truly magical experience. After descending, trek to Pheriche for the night. Trekking time: 7 hours.

Day 12

Trek to Tengboche

Begin the descent back through the Khumbu valley. Return to Tengboche and visit the monastery if you missed it on the way up. Enjoy the relative warmth of the lower altitude. Trekking time: 5 hours.

Day 13

Trek to Namche Bazaar

Continue descending to Namche Bazaar. Your body will feel much lighter as you return to lower altitudes. Enjoy your final night in the Sherpa capital and celebrate your achievement. Trekking time: 6 hours.

Day 14

Trek to Lukla

Complete your return journey to Lukla. This is your last night in the mountains, so celebrate with your team! Trekking time: 7 hours.

Day 15

Fly back to Kathmandu

Take the morning flight back to Kathmandu. Transfer to your hotel and enjoy a restful day. You can explore more of Kathmandu or simply relax after your epic adventure.

Day 16-18

Buffer days for weather delays

These days are reserved as buffers in case of flight delays due to weather conditions in Lukla. While flights typically run on schedule, having these days ensures you won't miss your international departure. If not needed, enjoy additional time exploring Kathmandu.

Day 19

Free day in Kathmandu

Free day for last-minute shopping, additional sightseeing, or simply relaxing. Visit Thamel's shops for souvenirs, Tibetan carpets, or local handicrafts. Optional visits to additional temples or museums.

Day 20

Departure

After breakfast, our representative will transfer you to Tribhuvan International Airport for your departure. Bid farewell to Nepal with memories that will last a lifetime!

Questions Answered

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about the Everest Base Camp trek.

How difficult is the Everest Base Camp trek?

The Everest Base Camp trek is considered a challenging trek suitable for people with a good level of fitness and stamina. While no technical climbing experience is required, you'll be walking 5-8 hours daily at high altitudes reaching 5,364m. The trails involve steep ascents and descents, and the thin air can make breathing difficult. Proper acclimatization days are built into the itinerary to help your body adjust.

Do I need prior trekking experience?

While prior trekking experience is beneficial, it's not strictly required for the Everest Base Camp trek. What's more important is having a good fitness level, mental preparedness, and willingness to endure physical challenges. If you're new to trekking, we recommend starting with shorter, easier treks like the Ghorepani Poon Hill trek to build your experience and confidence.

What is the accommodation like on the trek?

During the Everest Base Camp trek, you'll stay in teahouses - simple mountain lodges that are the standard accommodation along the trail. These teahouses offer basic but comfortable rooms, typically with two beds, blankets, and pillows. Common areas have a dining space where meals are served. While amenities are basic due to the remote location, teahouses provide a warm atmosphere with local hospitality.

Is altitude sickness a concern?

Altitude sickness (AMS) is a genuine concern on the Everest Base Camp trek due to the high elevations reached. However, with proper acclimatization and awareness, most trekkers adjust well. Our itinerary includes dedicated acclimatization days in Namche Bazaar and Dingboche to help your body adapt. Our guides are trained to recognize signs of AMS and will advise on appropriate measures.

When is the best time to do this trek?

The best times to do the Everest Base Camp trek are spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). Spring offers warmer temperatures, clear skies, and the famous rhododendron blooms in full color. Autumn provides stable weather, excellent visibility, and the festive atmosphere. We recommend booking 2-3 months in advance for peak seasons.

What should I pack for this trek?

Essential items include: layered clothing (base layers, insulating mid-layers, waterproof outer shell), comfortable trekking boots with good ankle support, warm down jacket, gloves, warm hat, and sunglasses with UV protection. Bring a good quality sleeping bag (rated to -15°C or colder), trekking poles for stability, and a daypack. Pack light - you'll only need about 8-10kg in your daypack.
