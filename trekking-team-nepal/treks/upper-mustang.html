Discover the Mystical Kingdom of Mustang

The Upper Mustang Trek offers a rare opportunity to explore one of Nepal's most isolated and culturally preserved regions. Known as the "Forbidden Kingdom," Upper Mustang was restricted to foreign visitors until 1992, and even today, it remains one of the most challenging and regulated areas to visit in Nepal.

This extraordinary 15-day journey takes you through dramatic desert-like landscapes, ancient Tibetan villages, and the legendary walled city of Lo Manthang. The region boasts a unique culture that has remained largely unchanged for centuries, with Buddhist monasteries, royal palaces, and traditional customs that offer a fascinating glimpse into a bygone era.

As you trek through the deep canyons and arid valleys of Upper Mustang, you'll be rewarded with breathtaking views of the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri mountain ranges. The highlight of the trek is Lo Manthang, a fortified city that served as the capital of the Kingdom of Mustang. Here, you can explore the 15th-century King's Palace, visit ancient monasteries, and witness the unique way of life that has been preserved through generations.

Itinerary

15-Day Trekking Adventure

1 Arrival in Kathmandu

+

Upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport, you will be warmly greeted by our representative and transferred to your hotel. In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner with a traditional Nepali cultural show. Later, receive a comprehensive trek briefing and prepare all necessary permits for the Upper Mustang region.

2 Fly to Pokhara

+

Take a scenic 25-minute flight from Kathmandu to Pokhara, offering breathtaking views of the Himalayas. Upon arrival, check into your hotel and spend the day preparing for the trek. You can also explore the beautiful Lakeside area, visit Phewa Lake, or relax with views of the Annapurna range.

3 Drive to Jomsom (2,720m)

+

Embark on a scenic 6-7 hour drive through the Kali Gandaki valley. The journey offers spectacular views of Dhaulagiri (8,167m) and Nilgiri (7,061m) mountains. Pass through traditional villages and small towns before reaching Jomsom, the district headquarters of Mustang.

4 Trek to Kagbeni (2,800m)

+

Begin your trek with a relatively easy 2-3 hour walk to Kagbeni. This medieval village marks the entrance to Upper Mustang and features narrow streets, whitewashed houses, and an ancient monastery. Complete the necessary police checkpoint formalities before entering the restricted area.

5 Trek to Chele (3,050m)

+

Today's 6-7 hour trek takes you through dramatic desert landscapes characterized by red cliffs, rock formations, and ancient caves. The trail ascends and descends through several small villages, offering glimpses of traditional Mustang culture. Camp or stay in local teahouses.

6 Trek to Syangboche (3,800m)

+

A challenging 6-7 hour trek awaits as you ascend through pine forests and rocky terrain. The trail passes through remote settlements and ancient cave dwellings. From the high points, enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Tonight's stop is at Syangboche.

7 Trek to Charang (3,500m)

+

Today's 5-6 hour trek passes through traditional villages with ancient walls and prayer walls. As you approach Charang, you'll get your first views of the walled city of Lo Manthang in the distance. Explore the village and its old monastery before settling in for the night.

8 Trek to Lo Manthang (3,820m)

+

The most anticipated day of the trek! A 5-6 hour walk brings you to the legendary walled city of Lo Manthang. Enter through the city gates and explore this ancient capital of the Kingdom of Mustang. The city is home to several monasteries, the King's Palace, and traditional Tibetan-style houses.

9 Explore Lo Manthang

+

Spend a full day exploring the wonders of Lo Manthang. Visit the Jampa Lhakhang (prayer wheel temple), the oldest building in the city housing a 28-meter tall Buddha statue. Explore the King's Palace, Thugchen monastery, and the intricate network of streets. Interact with local residents and learn about their unique way of life.

10 Trek to Yara (3,900m)

+

Today's 5-6 hour trek takes you to the village of Yara, famous for the mysterious fossil cave. The trail offers stunning views of dramatic cliffs and remote landscapes. Visit the fossil cave, believed to be 20 million years old and containing ancient marine fossils - a testament to the geological history of the region.

11 Trek back to Charang

+

Return trek to Charang via a different route, passing through remote villages and enjoying different perspectives of the landscape. Arrive in Charang by late afternoon and explore the village monastery if time permits.

12 Trek to Jomsom

+

A long 6-7 hour descent back to Jomsom. The trail passes through Kagbeni, where you exit the restricted area. Enjoy your last night in the Mustang region, celebrating the completion of this extraordinary journey.

13 Fly back to Kathmandu

+

Take the morning flight back to Kathmandu. Upon arrival, transfer to your hotel. Spend the rest of the day at leisure - you can explore Thamel, relax, or take an optional sightseeing tour of Kathmandu's UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

14 Buffer day

+

This buffer day is reserved as a contingency for any weather-related flight delays or unexpected circumstances. If everything goes according to plan, use this day for additional sightseeing, shopping for souvenirs, or simply relaxing and reflecting on your incredible journey.

15 Departure

+

According to your flight schedule, you will be transferred to Tribhuvan International Airport for your departure. Depart Nepal with unforgettable memories of your Upper Mustang adventure. We look forward to welcoming you back for your next Himalayan adventure!

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Upper Mustang Restricted Area Permit and how do I get it?

+

The Upper Mustang Restricted Area Permit is a special permit required to enter the Upper Mustang region, which was a restricted area until 1992. Foreign nationals need this permit to trek beyond Kagbeni. The permit can only be obtained through a registered trekking agency in Nepal, and the processing time is typically 2-3 weeks. As of 2024, the permit costs approximately $500 USD for 10 days (plus $50 per additional day). Trekking Team Nepal will arrange this permit for you as part of your package. You need to provide your passport copy and travel dates at least one month before your trek start date.

When is the best time to trek to Upper Mustang?

+

The best time to trek to Upper Mustang is during the spring months (March to May) and autumn months (September to November). Spring offers mild temperatures, blooming rhododendrons, and clear skies, making the desert-like landscape particularly stunning. Autumn provides crisp air, excellent visibility, and comfortable daytime temperatures. The summer monsoon season (June to August) brings heavy rainfall to the region and makes trekking conditions difficult. Winter (December to February) is extremely cold with heavy snowfall, making most trails inaccessible.

How difficult is the Upper Mustang Trek?

+

The Upper Mustang Trek is rated as moderate in difficulty. While no technical climbing experience is required, you should have a good fitness level and be prepared for 5-7 hours of daily walking. The trek involves walking on varied terrain including rocky trails, steep ascents and descents, and high-altitude paths. The highest point you'll reach is approximately 4,200 meters, which is lower than many other popular Nepal treks, reducing the risk of severe altitude sickness. However, you still need to acclimatize properly.

What makes Upper Mustang special compared to other Nepal treks?

+

Upper Mustang is often called the 'Forbidden Kingdom' because it remained isolated from the outside world until 1992. This isolation has preserved a unique Tibetan culture that has remained largely unchanged for centuries. The region features a distinctive desert-like landscape with red cliffs, deep canyons, and cave systems that are unlike any other trekking destination in Nepal. The highlight is Lo Manthang, a walled city that was the capital of the Kingdom of Mustang.

What should I pack for the Upper Mustang Trek?

+

Essential items include: sturdy trekking boots with good ankle support, warm layers (fleece, down jacket) for cold mornings and evenings, sun protection (sunglasses, hat, high-SPF sunscreen), a quality sleeping bag (rated to -10°C), trekking poles for steep sections, and a daypack. The air is dry and dusty, so bring lip balm and moisturizing cream. Include any personal medications, a basic first-aid kit, and water purification tablets. For the cultural aspects, bring modest clothing to wear in monasteries.

Can I combine the Upper Mustang Trek with other treks?

+

Yes, Upper Mustang can be combined with several other treks in the Annapurna region. The most popular combination is with the Annapurna Circuit Trek, as both routes share the Jomsom to Pokhara section. Another excellent option is combining Upper Mustang with the Lower Mustang trek. Some trekkers also choose to extend their journey to include the Tilicho Lake trek. These combinations typically add 5-10 days to your itinerary.
