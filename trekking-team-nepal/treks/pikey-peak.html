Discover the Best Everest Viewpoint

The Pikey Peak Trek offers one of the most spectacular and accessible viewpoints of Mount Everest without the extreme altitude challenges of higher treks. Located in the Solu Khumbu region of Nepal, this 10-day adventure takes you through pristine rhododendron forests, traditional Sherpa villages, and ancient Buddhist monasteries.

At 4,065 meters, Pikey Peak provides an unobstructed 360-degree view of the Himalayan range, including Everest, Lhotse, Makalu, and Kanchenjunga. The highlight of the trek is watching the sunrise from the summit, when the golden light transforms the mountains into a breathtaking panorama that will remain etched in your memory forever.

This trek is perfect for those seeking an introduction to high-altitude trekking or those who want to experience the majesty of Everest without the physical demands of longer expeditions. The route follows in the footsteps of legendary mountaineers, passing through the historic trail that led to the first Everest summit in 1953.

Detailed Itinerary

Your 10-Day Adventure

1Arrival in Kathmandu

+

Welcome to Nepal! Upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport, you will be greeted by our representative and transferred to your hotel. In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner with a traditional Nepali cultural show. Overnight in Kathmandu (1,300m).

2Drive to Dhap

+

After an early breakfast, we embark on a scenic drive from Kathmandu to Dhap. The journey takes us through the beautiful hills of Nepal, passing by traditional villages and terraced farmland. Dhap is the starting point of our trek and sits at an altitude of 2,850m. Overnight in Dhap. Driving Time: 8-9 hours

3Trek to Jhapre

+

Our trek begins! Today we walk through dense forests of pine, bamboo, and rhododendron. The trail ascends gently through small settlements, offering glimpses of local life. Jhapre is a small village with traditional houses and a small monastery. Overnight in Jhapre (2,830m). Trekking Time: 5-6 hours

4Trek to Pikey Base Camp

+

As we ascend higher, the vegetation changes from dense forest to alpine scrub. The trail offers increasingly spectacular views of the Himalayas. We reach Pikey Base Camp by late afternoon, giving us time to rest and acclimatize before the early morning summit push. Overnight at Pikey Base Camp (3,640m). Trekking Time: 5-6 hours

5Sunrise at Pikey Peak - Trek to Phakding

+

This is the highlight of the trip! Wake up before dawn and hike to the summit of Pikey Peak (4,065m) for an unforgettable sunrise experience. Watch as the first rays of sunlight illuminate Everest, Lhotse, Makalu, and the entire Himalayan range in golden light. After descending, we continue our trek to Phakding. Overnight in Phakding (2,610m). Trekking Time: 6-7 hours

6Explore Khumbu Region

+

Today we explore the famous Khumbu region, home to the legendary Sherpa people. Visit traditional Sherpa villages, explore ancient Buddhist monasteries, and immerse yourself in the rich culture of the Himalayas. Learn about the mountaineering history and the Sherpa people's relationship with the mountains. Overnight in Khumbu region.

7Return Trek - Day 1

+

Begin our return journey through the beautiful mountain trails. The descent is easier on the body, allowing us to appreciate the landscapes from a different perspective. We'll pass through villages we visited on the way up, giving us a chance to interact with locals again. Overnight in mountain village. Trekking Time: 5-6 hours

8Return Trek - Day 2

+

Continue descending through forests and villages. The trail follows river valleys and offers beautiful views of the surrounding hills. Arrive at Dhap by late afternoon. Overnight in Dhap. Trekking Time: 5-6 hours

9Drive to Kathmandu

+

After breakfast, we drive back to Kathmandu. The rest of the day is free for exploration, shopping in the famous Thamel market, or simply relaxing at the hotel. In the evening, enjoy a farewell dinner. Overnight in Kathmandu. Driving Time: 8-9 hours

10Departure

+

After breakfast, our representative will transfer you to Tribhuvan International Airport for your departure. Bid farewell to Nepal with memories that will last a lifetime!

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to do the Pikey Peak Trek?

The best time for Pikey Peak Trek is during spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November). These seasons offer stable weather, clear skies, and excellent mountain visibility. Spring brings rhododendron blooms, while autumn offers the most spectacular Everest views after the monsoon clears.

How difficult is the Pikey Peak Trek?

Pikey Peak Trek is rated as Easy to Moderate difficulty. It's suitable for beginners and families with children aged 10 and above. The maximum altitude of 4,065m is manageable with proper acclimatization. No prior trekking experience is required, but a reasonable level of fitness is recommended.

What is the maximum altitude on the Pikey Peak Trek?

The maximum altitude on Pikey Peak Trek is 4,065 meters (13,340 feet) at the Pikey Peak summit. This is lower than Everest Base Camp, making it an excellent option for those who want Everest views without the extreme altitude of higher treks.

Why is Pikey Peak known for the best Everest view?

Pikey Peak is renowned for having one of the best unobstructed views of Mount Everest. The peak offers a direct line-of-sight to Everest without any intervening mountains. The sunrise view from Pikey Peak is particularly spectacular, with the first rays of sunlight illuminating the entire Himalayan range including Everest, Lhotse, Makalu, and Kanchenjunga.

What permits do I need for Pikey Peak Trek?

For Pikey Peak Trek, you need two permits: Sagarmatha National Park Entry Permit (for entry into the Khumbu region) and TIMS (Trekkers' Information Management System) card. All permits are arranged by Trekking Team Nepal and included in the package price.

What should I pack for Pikey Peak Trek?

Essential items include warm layers, down jacket, trekking boots, sleeping bag, water bottle, sun protection, and basic first aid. The weather can change quickly in the mountains, so pack for both warm and cold conditions. A detailed packing list will be provided upon booking.
