Discover Nepal's Hidden Gem

The Ruby Valley Trek offers an extraordinary off-the-beaten-path adventure in the heart of Central Nepal. This 12-day journey takes you through pristine forests, remote villages, and breathtaking Himalayan landscapes, far from the crowded trails of more famous routes.

Named after the ruby mines found in the region, Ruby Valley remains one of Nepal's best-kept secrets. Trekkers here experience authentic rural Nepal culture, interact with friendly local communities, and witness spectacular mountain views including the majestic Ganesh Himal range.

Perfect for adventurous travelers seeking a unique trekking experience that combines natural beauty, cultural immersion, and physical challenge. The moderate difficulty level makes it accessible to those with reasonable fitness, while the remote location ensures a truly authentic adventure.

Day by Day

Ruby Valley Trek Itinerary

A carefully planned 12-day itinerary ensuring proper acclimatization and maximum adventure

1 Arrival in Kathmandu

+

Arrive at Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu. Our representative will greet you and transfer you to your hotel. In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner with traditional Nepali cultural show. Trek briefing and gear check will be provided. Overnight in Kathmandu at a 3-star hotel.

2 Drive to Dunthali

+

After breakfast, scenic drive from Kathmandu to Dunthali (approximately 5-6 hours). Enjoy the beautiful countryside landscapes, rural villages, and terraced fields along the way. Arrive in Dunthali in the afternoon. Evening at leisure to rest and acclimatize to the altitude. Overnight in Dunthali at a local guesthouse.

3 Trek to Chisapani

+

Begin your trekking adventure from Dunthali to Chisapani. The trail passes through dense forests, terraced fields, and small settlements. Enjoy pristine nature and glimpses of mountain views along the way. The trek takes approximately 5-6 hours. Overnight in Chisapani at a local teahouse.

4 Exploration Day at Chisapani

+

Rest day for acclimatization at Chisapani. Explore the surrounding villages, interact with local communities, and enjoy panoramic mountain views. Take optional short hikes to nearby viewpoints for stunning vistas of the Himalayan range. Overnight in Chisapani.

5 Trek to Dhargaon

+

Continue trekking from Chisapani to Dhargaon. The trail ascends through rhododendron forests and remote villages. Experience the unique culture of the local communities as you pass through traditional settlements. The trek takes approximately 6-7 hours. Overnight in Dhargaon at a local teahouse.

6 Acclimatization at Dhargaon

+

Acclimatization day at Dhargaon. Explore the village, visit local temples, and interact with the friendly locals. Take a short hike to nearby ridge for stunning mountain views. This rest day helps your body adjust to the altitude before reaching the highest point. Overnight in Dhargaon.

7 Trek to Ruby Valley

+

The main highlight of the trek - ascend from Dhargaon to Ruby Valley. Reach the beautiful Ruby Valley at 4,200m altitude. Enjoy spectacular Himalayan mountain views including Ganesh Himal and other peaks. The trek takes approximately 6-7 hours. Overnight in Ruby Valley at a basic teahouse.

8 Exploration at Ruby Valley

+

Full day to explore Ruby Valley and surrounding areas. Visit the famous ruby mines that give the valley its name. Enjoy breathtaking mountain scenery and immerse yourself in pristine nature. hike to higher viewpoints Optional for even more spectacular views. Overnight in Ruby Valley.

9 Return Trek to Dhargaon

+

Begin the return journey from Ruby Valley to Dhargaon. Enjoy a different perspective of the landscape as you descend through the valley. The trek takes approximately 5-6 hours. Evening free to relax and share experiences with fellow trekkers. Overnight in Dhargaon.

10 Return Trek to Chisapani

+

Continue return trek from Dhargaon to Chisapani. The trail offers different views as you descend. Evening free to relax and enjoy the mountain views. Share stories with other travelers. Overnight in Chisapani.

11 Return to Kathmandu

+

Trek from Chisapani back to Dunthali, then drive back to Kathmandu (approximately 5-6 hours drive). The rest of the day is free for shopping in Thamel or exploring Kathmandu's many attractions. Enjoy a farewell dinner with your group. Overnight in Kathmandu.

12 Departure

+

Transfer to Kathmandu International Airport for your departure flight. End of the Ruby Valley Trek adventure with memories of a lifetime. We hope to see you again for another adventure in beautiful Nepal!

Questions Answered

Frequently Asked Questions

? How difficult is the Ruby Valley Trek?

+

The Ruby Valley Trek is classified as Moderate difficulty. While no prior trekking experience is required, you should have a good level of physical fitness. The trek involves daily walking for 5-7 hours on varied terrain, including steep ascents and descents. The highest point reaches 4,200m, which requires proper acclimatization. Prior hiking experience and regular exercise before the trek are highly recommended.

? What is the best time to do the Ruby Valley Trek?

+

The best time for Ruby Valley Trek is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). Spring offers pleasant weather, rhododendron blooms in full color, and mild temperatures. Autumn provides clear skies, excellent visibility for mountain views, and comfortable walking conditions. Winter (December to February) can be extremely cold with snow at higher altitudes, while monsoon (June to August) brings heavy rainfall.

? What permits do I need for Ruby Valley Trek?

+

For the Ruby Valley Trek, you will need several permits including the Trekkers' Information Management System (TIMS) card and area permits for the region. All permits are arranged and provided by Trekking Team Nepal as part of your package. You do not need to obtain them separately - we handle all the paperwork for you before the trek begins.

? What makes Ruby Valley Trek special?

+

Ruby Valley Trek is a hidden gem offering an off-the-beaten-path experience far from the crowded trails of Everest and Annapurna. The trek showcases pristine nature, unique local culture, and spectacular Himalayan views including Ganesh Himal. It's perfect for adventurous travelers seeking authentic Nepal experiences without the tourist crowds. The valley is named after the ruby mines found in the area, adding historical interest to the journey.
