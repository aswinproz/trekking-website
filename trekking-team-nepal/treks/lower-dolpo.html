Detailed Itinerary

Your 20-day journey through the pristine Lower Dolpo region

Phase 1: Arrival and Getting to Dolpo

Day 1

Arrival in Kathmandu

Welcome to Nepal! Upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport, you will be greeted by our representative and transferred to your hotel. In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner with a traditional cultural show. Your trek leader will provide a detailed briefing about the upcoming adventure and help you prepare all necessary permits and equipment.

Day 2

Fly to Nepalgunj

Take an early morning flight from Kathmandu to Nepalgunj (approximately 1 hour). Nepalgunj is a vibrant border town in the western lowlands of Nepal. Use this day to explore the local market, prepare for the next day's flight to Juphal, and get a good night's rest before entering the mountain region.

Day 3

Fly to Juphal (2,400m) & Trek Preparation

Catch the morning flight from Nepalgunj to Juphal (approximately 20 minutes), which serves as the gateway to Dolpo. The flight offers spectacular views of the Himalayas. Upon arrival, meet your support team, organize your trekking gear, and prepare for the adventure ahead. Overnight near Juphal.

Phase 2: Trekking to Phoksundo Lake

Day 4-5

Trek to Dunai (2,850m)

Begin your trek following the Thuli Bheri River through lush forests and terraced fields. The trail gradually ascends to Dunai, the district headquarters of Dolpa. This 5-6 hour trek offers beautiful views of the surrounding hills and river valleys. Day 5 continues the journey to Dunai, where you can explore the village and prepare for the approach to Phoksundo Lake.

Day 6-7

Trek to Phoksundo Lake (3,600m)

The trail descends dramatically into the Phoksundo valley, offering your first views of the spectacular lake. The turquoise waters of Phoksundo Lake appear almost magical against the surrounding brown hills. These two days involve 6-7 hours of trekking through varied terrain, culminating in your arrival at one of Nepal's most beautiful lakes.

Day 8-9

Trek to Ringmo (3,700m)

After exploring Phoksundo Lake on Day 8, including visiting the nearby waterfall and enjoying photography opportunities, trek to the traditional village of Ringmo on Day 9. This charming village features ancient Buddhist chortens, prayer walls (mani walls), and friendly locals who maintain their traditional Tibetan Buddhist lifestyle.

Phase 3: Region Exploration

Day 10-12

Explore the Region

Spend three full days exploring the surrounding area. Visit local monasteries, interact with the friendly villagers, and immerse yourself in the unique culture of the region. Enjoy breathtaking mountain views, discover hidden temples, and appreciate the pristine natural beauty of Shey Phoksundo National Park. These days offer flexibility for photography, cultural experiences, and rest.

Phase 4: Return Journey

Day 13-16

Return Trek

Begin your return journey retracing your steps back through the beautiful Dolpo landscape. Different routes may be taken to provide varied views and experiences. These four days involve descending through the national park, passing through villages, and enjoying the changing perspectives of the mountains and valleys.

Day 17-20

Return to Kathmandu and Departure

Fly from Juphal to Nepalgunj and connect to Kathmandu. Day 18 serves as a buffer day in case of weather delays. Optional Day 19 allows for sightseeing in Kathmandu's UNESCO World Heritage sites or last-minute shopping in Thamel. Day 20 marks your departure from Nepal with unforgettable memories of your Lower Dolpo adventure.

Note: The itinerary may be adjusted slightly based on weather conditions, flight schedules, and group preferences. Your experienced trek leader will ensure the safest and most enjoyable experience possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the Lower Dolpo Trek

What is special about the Lower Dolpo Trek?

The Lower Dolpo Trek offers a unique opportunity to explore one of Nepal's most pristine and culturally rich regions without the strict permit requirements of Upper Dolpo. The trek features the stunning Phoksundo Lake, known for its remarkable turquoise waters, the ancient Ringmo village with its traditional Tibetan Buddhist culture, beautiful monasteries, and Shey Phoksundo National Park home to diverse wildlife.

Do I need special permits for the Lower Dolpo Trek?

Yes, the Lower Dolpo Trek requires several permits including: Lower Dolpo Restricted Area Permit (approximately $200 USD), Shey Phoksundo National Park Entry Permit (approximately $30 USD), and TIMS Card. All these permits can only be arranged through a registered trekking agency. Trekking Team Nepal will arrange all necessary permits for you as part of your package.

When is the best time to do the Lower Dolpo Trek?

The best time for the Lower Dolpo Trek is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). Spring offers pleasant temperatures and rhododendron forests in bloom. Autumn provides crisp, clear skies and excellent mountain views. Lower Dolpo lies in the rain shadow, making summer (June-August) also viable when the landscape is lush and green.

How difficult is the Lower Dolpo Trek?

The Lower Dolpo Trek is rated as moderate difficulty. The trek involves 14-16 days of actual trekking with daily hiking times of 4-7 hours. The highest point reaches 4,500m, which is lower than many other Nepal treks, reducing altitude sickness risk. Previous trekking experience is helpful but not mandatory. Regular walking and pre-trek conditioning will help ensure an enjoyable experience.

What are the main highlights of this trek?

Highlights include: Phoksundo Lake (the deepest lake in Nepal with turquoise waters), Ringmo Village (traditional Tibetan culture), Shey Phoksundo National Park (wildlife including blue sheep), ancient monasteries, pristine nature, and the scenic flight to Juphal with Himalayan views.

What should I pack for this trek?

Essential items include: sturdy trekking boots, warm layers (fleece, down jacket, windbreaker), rain gear, sun protection, sleeping bag (rated to -10°C), trekking poles, daypack (25-35L), water bottle, first aid kit, modest clothing for monasteries, and camera with extra batteries. Trekking Team Nepal provides a detailed packing list upon booking.
